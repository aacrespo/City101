Chapter 3

The Price of Distance

A GA annual pass costs CHF 3,860 — making the daily breakeven CHF 10.58. Beyond kilometer 15, every single trip costs more than the GA daily rate.

From CHF 3.60 (Geneva) to CHF 26.54 (Bex) — a 7.4× price difference. The GA pass creates a binary: those who have it access the entire corridor equally; those who don't face an exponentially rising cost barrier.
Chapter 4

The Diversity Fracture

Where diversity disappears, so does everything else. Four independent indices tell the same story.

Below Shannon diversity 1.0: average station richness is 8.8 amenities. Above 1.0: richness jumps to 70.5. The Lavaux segment sits almost entirely below the threshold — a systemic diversity collapse that mirrors every other metric.
Chapter 5

The Hierarchy Never Changes

Whether it's 6 AM or midnight, weekday or weekend — the same stations are always on top, and the same ones are always forgotten.

The Temporal Working Continuity Index across seven time periods reveals parallel lines that never cross. Genève stays at the top, St-Saphorin stays at the bottom. The hierarchy is not a peak-hour artifact — it is structural.

This is not a natural pattern.

It is a design choice.

49 stations, 5 segments, one corridor. The data tells a consistent story: access to working continuity along City101 is determined not by need, but by proximity to two urban cores.
