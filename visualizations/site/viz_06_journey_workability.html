The two-corridor paradox: All Prime connections are long-distance IC/IR trains (Geneva–Lausanne, Lausanne–Montreux). Short-hop S-Bahn connections — even between adjacent stations — are never prime. The same tracks carry two fundamentally different experiences: a workable linear city for IC passengers, and fragmented villages for everyone else.
