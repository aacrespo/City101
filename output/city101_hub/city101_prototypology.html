The Relay-Lock

A prototypology for the 24-hour healthcare supply chain along the Geneva–Villeneuve corridor.

Two concepts, one system

Relay — system level. A chain of 7 nodes along the corridor. Remove any node and the chain breaks. Each node depends on the others to keep the 24-hour city running.

Lock — building level. Architecture managing a threshold between two incompatible states, borrowed from canal lock logic. A chamber where transition happens.

The occupant is stationary — the space transitions around them. Like a canal lock where the boat stays still while water levels change.

Three scales

Infrastructure (Nodes 1, 4) — where healthcare cargo meets the city. Logistics hubs, sorting centers, the invisible backbone.

Staff access (Nodes 2, 3, 5, 7) — how 32,000 workers reach their shifts. The gap between transit and hospital.

Patient access (Nodes 2, 5, 6) — transfers, referrals, family visits. The human side of the chain.

The dead window

Between 00:30 and 05:00, there is zero weekday public transport on the entire 101 km corridor. All 8 supply chain categories become 100% road-dependent. 4,600–6,300 healthcare workers are stranded or driving. The chamber — the architectural space of the lock — is most needed precisely when the city shuts down.
