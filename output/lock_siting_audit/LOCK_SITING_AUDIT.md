# LOCK SITING AUDIT — All 9 Nodes

**Date:** 2026-03-18
**Status:** Complete (Phase 1-3)
**Branch:** andrea/prototypology-v2

---

## Executive Summary

This audit applies the Morges discovery — **start from the person, not the geometry** — to all 9 relay-lock nodes. Three parallel transit research agents investigated weekday night transit for every node. The findings reshape several nodes fundamentally.

### Top Findings

1. **CHUV (Node 6) is the most critical node on the corridor.** 2,500-3,000 workers stranded on a steep hillside when the M2 stops at 00:45 Mon-Thu. The gradient IS the gap. CHUV's response: subsidized parking (CHF 56/month).

2. **Rennaz (Node 9) is the most acute transit desert.** 200-300 hospital night staff face a 5h52m bus dead window, no rail station, and a 2.1km walk along a cantonal highway. Citrap-vaud has advocated for a fix since 2009.

3. **Lancy-Pont-Rouge (Node 1) is NOT a dead-window problem.** Frontaliers are home in France. French nurses at HUG are locked into hospital shifts 3.5km away. The station area is empty at 02:00. The lock concept needs reframing.

4. **Crissier (Node 5) is structurally different.** The gap is spatial (car-only infrastructure), not temporal. Even 24/7 rail wouldn't fix the 2.5km last-mile through an industrial zone.

5. **The universal institutional response to night gaps is: drive.** Every hospital, clinic, and logistics center provides parking. None provide night shuttles.

6. **Monday-Thursday is the real crisis.** Noctambus runs Fri/Sat only. The audit focuses on weekday dead windows.

## The 9 Node Cards

### NODE 1 — Lancy-Pont-Rouge — BORDER LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Marie, 34, HUG ER nurse (frontalier from Annemasse). At 02:00: on shift at HUG, 3.5km from Lancy-Pont-Rouge. The station is empty — 1,800 residents sleeping.
**TRANSIT:** Rail dead window 01:07-04:07 (3h00m). TPG trams/buses until ~01:00 (Dec 2025 extension). No Noctambus Mon-Thu. No cross-border night service.
**GAP:** Not a dead-window problem. The 160,000 frontaliers are home by evening. The ~3,200 French nurses at HUG are locked into hospital shifts. The real gap is at **shift boundaries** (22:00 and 05:30) when transit runs out and workers must commit to staying or can't get home.
**SITING:** Reframe. Consider moving to Champel-Hôpital or HUG perimeter, where stranded workers actually are. If staying at Lancy-PR, reorient to 05:00-07:00 shift-change rush.
**MODEL NEEDS:** HUG campus, Champel-Hôpital station, temporal border visualization.
**STATUS:** ⚠️ NEEDS REVISION — lock concept needs reframing

### NODE 2 — Geneva North Industrial — CARGO LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Amina, 29, pharma warehouse operator at ZIMEYSA. Shift ends at 02:00. She walks 200m to the parking lot. Paulo, 42, Swissport ground handler, starts at 04:00 — drives because the bus starts at 04:32.
**TRANSIT:** Rail at Airport 00:19-04:58 (4h39m). Bus 57 00:24-04:32 (4h08m). Aerobus 03:00-06:00 but requires boarding pass (workers can't use it). Airport NPA shuttle exists (limited, unclear coverage).
**GAP:** 4h08m of zero public transit for workers without boarding passes. Split geography: airport workers (inside security perimeter) vs. ZIMEYSA workers (2-3km away, industrial roads, no pedestrian infrastructure).
**SITING:** At the interface between airport security perimeter and ZIMEYSA industrial zone — the no-man's-land where workers cross between the two worlds.
**MODEL NEEDS:** Airport terminal footprint, ZIMEYSA warehouses, the gap between them, Bus 57 route.
**STATUS:** 🔲 NOT YET MODELED

### NODE 3 — Nyon-Genolier — ALTITUDE LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Sophie, 26, junior nurse at Clinique de Genolier. No car. NStCM last train to hilltop: ~21:29. She taxis (CHF 40) or gets colleague rides. On night shifts, she stays at the clinic — there is no way down.
**TRANSIT:** NStCM to Genolier: dead window ~8h (21:29-05:37). SBB at Nyon: 3h18m (01:05-04:23). No clinic shuttle. Taxi CHF 35-45 with night surcharge.
**GAP:** 8 hours of altitude severance. Not a dead window — an altitude prison. Walking: 6.2km, 200m descent, unlit rural roads through forest. Impossible at night.
**SITING:** At NStCM Genolier station ("Genolier clinique/campus") — the altitude boundary where transit stops. The lock sits where the gate closes.
**MODEL NEEDS:** Genolier hilltop (clinic, NStCM station), Nyon valley (station), the descent road, two-endpoint story.
**STATUS:** 🔲 NOT YET MODELED

### NODE 4 — Morges — TEMPORAL LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Aline, 31, ICU nurse at EHC Morges. Relief shift ends at 02:00. Last bus (MBC 704) left hospital at 00:07. Last train at 01:07. She waits in the hospital break room until 03:30, then walks 15 min through empty town streets to the station.
**TRANSIT:** Rail 01:07-04:01 (2h54m — shortest mid-corridor). MBC 704 from hospital: 00:07-~05:30 (5h23m at hospital). No EHC shuttle. EHC mobility plan calls transit "virtually non-existent" for night workers.
**GAP:** 3h54m from last bus (00:07) to first train (04:01). The walk is 1.25km, flat, 15-20 min through town center. Feasible but isolating at 02:00.
**SITING:** Near Gare de Morges, **town side** (Z≈376, not rail embankment Z≈381.5). The lock catches the nurse at the destination, not the origin. The hospital already provides shelter.
**MODEL NEEDS:** EHC Morges (50m outside tile — EXTEND NORTH), walking route, station at town level AND track level (6m embankment split), lock at town level.
**STATUS:** ⚠️ NEEDS REVISION — Z wrong (rail vs. town), hospital outside tile

### NODE 5 — Crissier-Bussigny — LOGISTICS ENGINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Yusuf, 38, forklift operator at Feldschlösschen, Crissier. Night shift 22:00-06:00. Drove to work — always has. Bus 18 stops at 23:46, the logistics zone has no pedestrian infrastructure.
**TRANSIT:** Rail at Bussigny: 00:55-04:48 (3h53m). TL Bus 18: ~23:46-~05:30 (~5h44m). No employer shuttle found (likely exist but invisible to public data).
**GAP:** Structural, not temporal. Distribution centers built for trucks, not transit. 2.5km from Bussigny station through hostile industrial landscape. Even 24/7 rail wouldn't fix the last-mile problem.
**SITING:** At the logistics zone edge — where industrial meets public road. A "visibility lock" that reveals invisible night labor.
**MODEL NEEDS:** Warehouse footprints, truck infrastructure, the edge condition, Bussigny station as distant context.
**STATUS:** 🔲 NOT YET MODELED

### NODE 6 — Lausanne CHUV — GRADIENT DISPATCHER
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Fatima, 44, CHUV lab technician. Shift ends at 02:00. She stands at the M2 CHUV entrance — doors locked, escalators dark, station empty. 90m of steep hill between her and the Gare. In January, icy. She waits in the hospital lobby until 05:15.
**TRANSIT:** M2 Mon-Thu: ~00:45-~05:15 (4h30m gap). Rail: 00:34-04:38 (4h04m). Lausanne-Flon: 00:36-05:40 (**5h04m — longest single-mode gap on corridor**). No CHUV night shuttle Mon-Wed. Night parking CHF 56/month.
**GAP:** The M2 does NOT run at 02:00 (confirmed). 2,500-3,000 workers stranded on steep hillside. The gradient (90m over 1km, steep streets, stairs) IS the gap. CHUV's institutional response: subsidized car parking.
**SITING:** Adjacent to M2 CHUV station entrance — the locked door that embodies the gap. The lock should transition from "transit lobby" to "night refuge" at 00:45 when the last M2 departs.
**MODEL NEEDS:** CHUV campus (132m outside tile — EXTEND), M2 station entrance, the gradient descent, Lausanne Gare as destination endpoint, steep streets/stairs.
**STATUS:** ⚠️ NEEDS REVISION — hospital outside tile, M2 now confirmed dead

### NODE 7 — Vevey — GAP RELAY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Marco, 28, hotel night receptionist. Walked 5 min from his apartment. No transit problem. The real vulnerability: anyone in Chardonne/Mont-Pèlerin after the 00:07 funicular is stranded on the hillside.
**TRANSIT:** Rail: 01:04-05:01 (3h57m). VMCV bus: ~midnight-~05:25 (~5h25m). VCP funicular to Chardonne: 00:07-05:32 (5h25m). No institutional shuttles. Nestle = daytime office only.
**GAP:** 3h57m (moderate). Night population sparse: 20-40 scattered hotel/hospitality workers. Vevey's value is positional — 13km gap relay across Lavaux Fracture.
**SITING:** Vevey Gare — a lightweight "swing station" relay, not a full shelter. Waypoint for home care network (AVASAD). Information kiosk, warm waiting, dispatch board.
**MODEL NEEDS:** Vevey Gare, lakefront, VCP funicular station, 13km gap context, lightweight lock.
**STATUS:** 🔲 NOT YET MODELED

### NODE 8 — Montreux-Glion — ALTITUDE LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Dr. Nguyen, 39, neurologist at Clinique Valmont, Glion. On night call. The funicular stopped at 01:11. She's at the clinic, 300m above the lake, on a dark mountain road. 15-25 night staff here.
**TRANSIT:** Rail at Montreux: 00:56-04:43 (3h47m). **Territet-Glion funicular: 01:11-05:11 (4h00m dead window at altitude)**. Key finding: funicular runs 15 min AFTER last train — narrow buffer exists. Glion-Rochers rack = daytime only.
**GAP:** 4h00m of altitude severance after 01:11. But the funicular runs later than expected — Glion is not fully severed until 01:11. 15-25 clinic staff locked at altitude in dark, steep environment. Hotel night workers at lakefront have no altitude issue.
**SITING:** At Territet-Glion funicular base station — the "lock gate" where altitude severance begins. The lock marks the threshold between "connected" and "severed."
**MODEL NEEDS:** Territet funicular base, funicular track to Glion, Clinique Valmont footprint, Montreux lakefront hotels as context, altitude section (375m to 675m).
**STATUS:** 🔲 NOT YET MODELED

### NODE 9 — Rennaz — BRIDGE LOCK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
**PERSON:** Thomas, 24, junior nurse at HRC. Drove to his 22:00 shift because the last bus was at 23:53. HRC has 1,965 employees but only 912 parking spaces. At 02:00, he's on the acute care ward. Outside: Route 9, truck traffic from the A9, darkness, no bus until 05:45.
**TRANSIT:** Bus 201 at HRC: 23:53-05:45 (**5h52m — longest institutional dead window**). Rail at Villeneuve (2.1km): 00:51-04:57 (4h06m). No rail at Rennaz. No hospital shuttle. Simplon railway passes 500m from HRC without stopping.
**GAP:** 5h52m of zero transit at the hospital itself. 200-300 night staff, inadequate parking, 2.1km cantonal highway walk to Villeneuve with truck traffic and limited lighting. Citrap-vaud advocating since 2009.
**SITING:** On the Villeneuve-HRC axis, at midpoint or closer to hospital. A literal bridge spanning the hostile gap between rail and hospital.
**MODEL NEEDS:** HRC hospital (644m outside tile — CRITICAL), Villeneuve station, Route 9 highway, A9 motorway, the Simplon rail line passing without stopping, the lock on the bridge axis.
**STATUS:** ⚠️ NEEDS REVISION — hospital far outside tile
