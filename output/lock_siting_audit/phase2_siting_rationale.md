## Node 1 — Lancy-Pont-Rouge (km 4) — Border Lock

### The Person
**Marie, 34, HUG emergency nurse.** Lives in Annemasse, France. Crossed the border at 19:30 for her night shift. The last Léman Express back to France left at ~00:30. She is committed to staying until her shift ends at 06:00. She is not stranded at Lancy-Pont-Rouge — she is locked into the hospital 3.5 km away.

### At 02:00
Marie is on the third floor of HUG's emergency department, Rue Gabrielle-Perret-Gentil. The station at Lancy-Pont-Rouge is empty — 1,800 residents are sleeping. The border crossing at Bardonnex is physically open but there is no transit to reach it. Marie's car is in the staff parking garage because she learned years ago that public transit can't be trusted after midnight.

### The Gap
The gap is not at 02:00 — it's at the **transition boundaries**. At 22:00, colleagues arriving for the night shift after last cross-border service must have already committed to being in Geneva all night. At 05:30-06:00, Marie finishes and needs to get back to France, but regular service hasn't fully ramped up. The "border" isn't a physical line — it's a temporal one. After ~00:30, the border becomes one-way: you can cross by car, but you can't cross back by transit until morning.

### Siting Recommendation
**Re-site away from Lancy-Pont-Rouge station. Consider Champel-Hôpital (Léman Express station) or the HUG campus perimeter.**

Rationale: The people this lock serves (frontalier night nurses) are at HUG, not at Lancy-Pont-Rouge. The station area is a residential development that is empty at night. The "border" concept should be reframed as a **temporal border** — the moment when transit runs out and night workers must commit to staying. The lock should be where workers make that commitment: near the hospital, not near a dormant station.

**Alternative framing:** If the lock must remain at Lancy-Pont-Rouge (as the CEVA/Léman Express hub for cross-border connectivity), its program should focus on the 05:00-07:00 shift-change rush when ~3,200 frontalier healthcare workers need to cross back to France — not on the 02:00 dead window.

### Model Needs
- HUG campus and Champel-Hôpital station context
- The temporal border visualization: a timeline showing when cross-border transit dies and revives
- Lancy-Pont-Rouge only if the lock stays here — in which case, show the emptiness at 02:00 vs. the 06:00 rush

### Status: NEEDS REVISION — lock concept needs reframing

## Node 2 — Geneva North Industrial Belt (km 8) — Cargo Lock

### The Person
**Paulo, 42, Swissport ground handler at GVA.** His shift starts at 04:00 to prep for the 06:15 Zurich flight. He drives because Bus 57 doesn't run until 04:32 — too late. His colleague **Amina, 29, warehouse operator at ZIMEYSA**, handles pharma cold-chain logistics. Her shift ends at 02:00. She walks 200m to the warehouse parking lot, gets in her car, and drives home to Vernier. There is no bus.

### At 02:00
Paulo is at home, setting his alarm for 03:00. Amina is finishing her shift at a ZIMEYSA warehouse 2.5 km from the airport terminal. The airport itself hums with security, cleaning crews, and cargo handlers inside the security perimeter. The NPA employee shuttle may still be running (unclear), but it serves the airport platform, not ZIMEYSA. Between the airport terminal and the ZIMEYSA warehouses: 2-3 km of dark industrial road with no sidewalk.

### The Gap
**4h08m of zero public transit** (00:24 to 04:32) for anyone without a boarding pass. The Aerobus runs from 03:00, but it requires an airline ticket — airport WORKERS can't use it. The split geography is the key problem: airport workers are inside a security perimeter, ZIMEYSA workers are in an industrial zone, and between them is a pedestrian desert.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at the interface between airport security perimeter and ZIMEYSA industrial zone — the no-man's-land where workers cross between the two worlds.**

Rationale: The cargo lock's value is in bridging the split geography. Workers at the airport platform have institutional support (NPA shuttle, security-controlled environment). Workers at ZIMEYSA have nothing — they're in warehouses designed for trucks. The lock should occupy the threshold between these two zones, providing a gathering/dispatch point for shift changes and a safe pedestrian connection through the industrial landscape.

### Model Needs
- Airport terminal footprint (security perimeter boundary)
- ZIMEYSA warehouse zone (major buildings)
- Route between them (roads, lack of pedestrian infrastructure)
- The lock at the interface point
- Bus 57 route for context

### Status: NOT YET MODELED

## Node 3 — Nyon-Genolier (km 25) — Altitude Lock

### The Person
**Sophie, 26, junior nurse at Clinique de Genolier.** She doesn't have a car — she moved to Nyon 6 months ago and takes the NStCM train up the hill. But the last NStCM to Genolier leaves at ~21:29, and her evening shift doesn't end until 22:00. She takes a taxi (CHF 40 with night surcharge) or gets a ride from a colleague. When she works nights, she stays at the clinic — there is literally no way down.

### At 02:00
Sophie is on the second floor of Clinique de Genolier, a private hospital on a hilltop at 550m altitude. Below her, Nyon is 200m down and 6 km away. The NStCM stopped 4.5 hours ago. The road down is dark, winding, through forest, with no sidewalk. Her car-owning colleagues are parked in the clinic's hilltop lot. She can see the lights of Nyon and Lake Geneva below, but she cannot reach them.

### The Gap
**8 hours of complete transit severance** (21:29 to 05:37) between the hilltop clinic and the valley. This is not a "dead window" — it's an altitude prison. The NStCM shuts down 4 hours before the mainline rail at Nyon, making the real gap 2.4x worse than the corridor average. Walking is genuinely dangerous: 6 km of steep, unlit, rural road.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at the NStCM Genolier station (P+R "Genolier clinique/campus") — the altitude boundary where transit stops.**

Rationale: The lock sits where the lock gate closes. The NStCM station at Genolier is the threshold between "connected to the valley" and "severed at altitude." At 21:30, this gate closes and the hilltop becomes an island. The lock should serve as the last-resort connection point: shuttle dispatch, taxi coordination, safe waiting, and a visible marker that the system knows people are up here.

The lock should NOT be in Nyon (the valley) — the stranded workers are at altitude, not in town. The lock should be at the point of severance, adjacent to the clinic.

### Model Needs
- Genolier hilltop: clinic, NStCM station, P+R, the road down
- Nyon valley: station, town center (as distant context)
- The descent: road trajectory, forest, elevation change
- Two-endpoint story: hilltop clinic AND valley station

### Status: NOT YET MODELED

## Node 4 — Morges (km 48) — Temporal Lock

### The Person
**Jean-Marc, 55, night security guard at EHC Morges.** His shift ends at 06:00 — he'll catch the 06:08 train. But **Aline, 31, ICU nurse**, finishes her relief shift at 02:00. The last bus (MBC 704) left the hospital at 00:07. The last train left at 01:07. She has a 2h54m wait until the first train at 04:01. She can walk the 1.25 km to the station in 15 minutes, but the town is dead. She usually sits in the hospital break room until 03:30, then walks down.

### At 02:00
Aline is in the EHC Morges break room, Avenue de la Gottaz. Outside, the streets of Morges are empty. The walk to the station goes through the town center — Rue de la Gare, the main commercial street — but every shop is shuttered. The rail tracks sit on an embankment at Z=381, while the town and walking route are 6m below at Z=375-378. The station is a 15-minute walk through silence.

### The Gap
**3h54m from last bus (00:07) to first train (04:01).** The rail dead window itself is only 2h54m (one of the shortest on the corridor), but the hospital-to-station connection dies an hour earlier. EHC's own mobility plan calls public transit "virtually non-existent" for night workers and responds with parking permits.

### Siting Recommendation
**Keep the lock near Gare de Morges — but shift emphasis from the rail platform to the town-side arrival point (Rue de la Gare / Place de la Gare, Z≈375-378).**

Rationale: Morges is the "purest" temporal lock — the gap is cleanly temporal, the walk is short and flat, and the person is clearly identifiable. The lock should be at the station end (not the hospital end) because:
1. Aline walks DOWN from the hospital — the lock catches her at the destination, not the origin
2. The station is the gateway to the corridor — the lock connects to the first train
3. Other night workers from Morges town (hotels, restaurants) converge on the station
4. The hospital already provides shelter (break room) — the station does not

**Critical change from current model:** The lock should be at TOWN LEVEL (Z≈375-378), not on the rail embankment (Z≈381.5). The current SITE_ORIGIN at Z=381.5 puts the lock at track level, disconnected from the pedestrian experience. The walk happens 6m below the tracks.

### Model Needs
- The hospital (currently 50m outside terrain tile — MUST extend north)
- The walking route through town (Av. Muret → town center → Rue de la Gare)
- The station at town level AND track level (showing the 6m embankment split)
- The lock at town level, oriented toward the walking approach
- Both endpoints: hospital origin AND station destination

### Status: NEEDS REVISION — Z level wrong, hospital outside tile

## Node 5 — Crissier-Bussigny-Ecublens (km 58-62) — Logistics Engine

### The Person
**Yusuf, 38, forklift operator at Feldschlösschen distribution platform, Crissier.** His shift runs 22:00-06:00. He drove to work because Bus 18 stopped at 23:46 and he needed to arrive at 21:45. At 02:00, he's moving pallets of beer to staging areas for morning delivery trucks. He has never taken public transit to work for a night shift — it has never been possible.

### At 02:00
Yusuf is inside a massive warehouse at Crissier's logistics zone. Outside: loading docks, truck parking, Route de Cossonay with 40-ton vehicles. The nearest train station (Bussigny) is 2.5 km away across an industrial landscape designed for trucks. The roads have no sidewalks in the warehouse areas. Even the bus stops (TL 18, 36) are along main roads, not inside the logistics zone.

### The Gap
**Structural, not temporal.** Even if trains ran 24/7, Yusuf wouldn't use them — the 2.5 km from Bussigny station to his warehouse, through an industrial zone at night, is not a transit-compatible journey. The gap is that the entire logistics zone was built for truck access, with zero pedestrian or transit infrastructure reaching the actual workplaces. Employer shuttles likely exist but are invisible to public data.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at the logistics zone edge — where the industrial zone meets the public road network — as an exposed "machine room."**

Rationale: This is a "visibility lock" — the architecture makes visible what is normally invisible. The lock doesn't solve a transit gap (these workers drive). It reveals that hundreds of people work through the night in spaces designed to hide them. The lock should be at the edge of the logistics zone, on Route de Cossonay or Chemin du Closalet, where truck infrastructure meets human-scale architecture. It functions as:
1. A shift-change gathering point (beginning/end of shift)
2. A marker of night labor (architectural visibility)
3. A potential future transit node (if shuttle/bus service ever extends here)

### Model Needs
- Warehouse footprints (Feldschlösschen, DHL, others)
- Truck infrastructure (loading docks, parking, Route de Cossonay)
- The edge condition: where industrial meets public
- Bussigny station as distant context
- The lock at the zone boundary

### Status: NOT YET MODELED

## Node 6 — Lausanne CHUV (km 65) — Gradient Dispatcher

### The Person
**Fatima, 44, CHUV lab technician.** She processes blood samples for the night ER. Her shift ends at 02:00. The M2 metro stopped at ~00:45. She stands at the CHUV metro entrance — the doors are locked, the escalators dark. The station below her feet is empty. She looks down the hill toward Lausanne Gare, 90m below, through steep streets that are icy in January. She has two choices: walk down the hill in the dark, or wait in the hospital lobby until 05:15 when the M2 restarts.

### At 02:00
Fatima is at CHUV, Rue du Bugnon, elevation ~540m. 2,500-3,000 other night workers surround her in the hospital complex. The M2 station "CHUV" is closed — glass doors locked, no lights. The city of Lausanne spreads below: the Gare at 450m, the lake at 373m. The gradient that the M2 was built to conquer has reconquered the hillside. A walk to the station means 90m of steep descent through Rue du Pont, Place de la Riponne (empty, sometimes sketchy), Rue Centrale. In winter, these streets can be treacherous with ice.

### The Gap
**~4h30m of M2 darkness** (00:45 to 05:15, Mon-Thu). The Lausanne-Flon dead window (5h04m) is the longest single-mode gap on the entire corridor. During this time, the largest concentration of night workers in the canton (2,500-3,000) is stranded on a steep hillside. CHUV's institutional response: subsidized night parking at CHF 56/month.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at or immediately adjacent to the M2 CHUV station entrance — the locked door that embodies the gap.**

Rationale: The locked M2 station IS the lock. The gradient dispatcher concept is physically manifested by the metro that stops running. The lock should:
1. Occupy the space around the M2 entrance — the threshold where workers discover they're stranded
2. Provide what the dead M2 cannot: a way to descend (shuttle dispatch), a reason to stay (rest, work space), and certainty (information about when the system restarts)
3. Address the gradient directly — not as a horizontal distance but as a vertical barrier

The current 4-level gradient dispatcher concept is architecturally sound but needs to respond to the M2 closure specifically. The lock's program should CHANGE at 00:45 — it transitions from "transit lobby" to "night refuge" when the last M2 departs.

### Model Needs
- CHUV campus (currently 132m outside terrain tile — MUST extend)
- M2 station entrance and the locked doors
- The gradient: from CHUV elevation down to Gare
- The walking route (steep streets, stairs)
- The lock at the M2 threshold, responding to the gradient
- Both endpoints: CHUV (where workers are) AND Gare (where they need to go)

### Status: NEEDS REVISION — hospital outside tile, M2 hours now confirmed

## Node 7 — Vevey (km 80) — Gap Relay

### The Person
**Marco, 28, night receptionist at Hôtel Astra, Vevey.** His shift runs 23:00-07:00. He walked to work from his apartment near the station — 5 minutes. At 02:00, he's alone at the front desk, monitoring security cameras and handling occasional guest requests. When his shift ends, he walks home. He has no transit problem. But **Claire, 52, home care nurse (AVASAD)**, finished her last visit at 00:30 in La Tour-de-Peilz and needs to get back to her car parked at Vevey station. The last bus was at midnight.

### At 02:00
Vevey's lakefront is quiet. The Nestle campus is dark (offices, not 24h operations). A scattering of hotel receptionists are at their posts. The real vulnerability is above: residents and workers in Chardonne and Mont-Pèlerin, where the VCP funicular stopped at 00:07. An estimated 20-40 people are working across Vevey at this hour — too few for a dedicated architectural intervention.

### The Gap
**3h57m rail dead window** — moderate. But the night population is sparse and dispersed. Vevey's value in the network is positional: it fills the 13km gap between CHUV (Node 6) and Montreux (Node 8). Without Vevey, the eastern half of the corridor has no relay point across the Lavaux Fracture.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at Vevey Gare — a lightweight relay, not a full shelter.**

Rationale: Vevey doesn't need a "home station" (like Morges). It needs a "swing station" — a minimal relay point where the corridor's continuity is maintained. The lock should:
1. Function as a waypoint for the home care network (AVASAD workers traversing the Riviera)
2. Provide the relay function: information, safe waiting, connection to neighboring nodes
3. Mark the corridor's presence across the Lavaux gap
4. Serve as the funicular interface for Mont-Pèlerin/Chardonne (secondary function)

The program is lighter than other nodes: information kiosk, warm waiting space, charging points, dispatch board. Not a multi-level chamber.

### Model Needs
- Vevey Gare and lakefront context
- Funicular station (VCP) as altitude connection
- The 13km gap visualization (Lavaux Fracture context)
- Lightweight lock at station

### Status: NOT YET MODELED

## Node 8 — Montreux-Glion (km 85) — Altitude Lock

### The Person
**Dr. Nguyen, 39, neurologist at Clinique Valmont, Glion.** She is on night call — if a patient's condition changes, she responds. At 01:05, she took the second-to-last funicular down to Territet to grab food. The funicular stops at 01:11. She's back at the clinic now, 300m above the lake, on a dark mountain road. The 15-25 other night staff are here too. The rack railway to Rochers-de-Naye stopped at 16:27. Above Glion, everything is severed.

### At 02:00
Dr. Nguyen is at Clinique Valmont, Glion. The funicular is dead — last car went down at 01:11. The road to Territet is 1.2 km of steep, unlit mountain switchbacks with no sidewalk in places. Below, Montreux's lakefront hotels have 30-50 night staff working, but they're at lake level with no transit issues beyond the standard dead window. The altitude creates two distinct worlds: lakefront (uncomfortable but manageable) and Glion (stranded).

### The Gap
**4h00m of altitude severance** (01:11 funicular to 05:11 first funicular). Key finding: the funicular runs until 01:11 — later than the last train at Montreux (00:56) — so there's a 15-minute buffer where a last-train passenger can still ascend. But after 01:11, the gate closes absolutely.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site at the Territet-Glion funicular base station — the altitude lock gate.**

Rationale: The funicular base station at Territet is where the "lock gate" closes. It is the physical threshold between "connected to the corridor" (lakefront rail) and "severed at altitude" (Glion clinics). The lock should:
1. Mark the moment of severance: after 01:11, you cannot ascend
2. Serve as dispatch point for Glion: last-chance coordination for clinic staff
3. Interface with the Territet rail stop (200m walk along lakefront)
4. Tell the story of vertical severance through architecture

The lock should NOT be at Glion (too isolated, too few people to justify full infrastructure) but at the base, where the altitude transition is controlled. This mirrors the canal lock principle: the gate is at the bottom, not the top.

### Model Needs
- Territet funicular base station
- The funicular track ascending to Glion (steep, through forest)
- Glion: Clinique Valmont footprint
- Montreux lakefront (station, hotels) as context
- The altitude section: from lake level (~375m) to Glion (~675m)
- Two endpoints: Territet base AND Glion hilltop

### Status: NOT YET MODELED

## Node 9 — Rennaz (km 89) — Bridge Lock

### The Person
**Isabelle, 37, ER nurse at Hôpital Riviera-Chablais (HRC).** She finishes her night shift at 06:00. The first bus (VMCV 201) from the hospital arrives at 05:45. She could catch it if she rushes out. But her colleague **Thomas, 24, junior nurse**, started his shift at 22:00. He drove because the last bus was at 23:53 and he needed to arrive at 21:45 for handover. HRC has 1,965 employees but only 912 parking spaces — 515 for staff. Thomas parks in the overflow lot across the road.

### At 02:00
Thomas is on the acute care ward at HRC Rennaz, a modern hospital building in the Rhône plain. Outside, the cantonal Route 9 carries occasional truck traffic from the A9 motorway. The nearest train station (Villeneuve) is 2.1 km west, along this highway. There is no sidewalk for portions of the route. The bus shelter at "Rennaz, Hôpital" is dark — the last bus left 2 hours ago, the next won't come for nearly 4 more hours. 200-300 night staff are inside the hospital. The Simplon railway passes within 500m — trains don't stop.

### The Gap
**5h52m of zero transit at the hospital itself** (23:53 to 05:45). The longest dead window of any node with a major institution. No rail station at Rennaz. No hospital shuttle. Insufficient parking (912 spaces for 1,965 employees). The 2.1 km walk to Villeneuve follows a cantonal highway with truck traffic — unsafe at 02:00. Citrap-vaud has been advocating for better access since 2009.

### Siting Recommendation
**Site on the 2.1 km axis between Villeneuve station and HRC — at the midpoint or closer to the hospital.**

Rationale: The "bridge lock" is literally a bridge — it spans the hostile gap between rail network and hospital. The lock should:
1. Create a safe, lit, human-scale path through the highway landscape
2. Provide a gathering/dispatch point at or near the hospital
3. Orient along the Villeneuve-HRC axis (the "bridge" direction)
4. Make the 2.1 km gap visible as an architectural problem, not just an infrastructure complaint

The lock concept (off-rail corrective) is validated: this IS a bridge between two disconnected systems. The current model should verify its orientation along this axis.

### Model Needs
- HRC hospital (currently 644m outside terrain tile — CRITICAL extension needed)
- Villeneuve station
- The 2.1 km Route 9 between them (highway character, lack of pedestrian infrastructure)
- The A9 motorway context
- The Simplon railway passing without stopping
- The lock on the axis, bridging the gap
- Both endpoints: Villeneuve station AND HRC

### Status: NEEDS REVISION — hospital far outside tile, axis orientation needs verification
