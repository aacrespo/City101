### Dead Window Duration

**Rail-only dead window:** 01:07 – 04:07 = **3h00m** (from existing CSV data, HIGH confidence)

**All-mode dead window (estimated):** ~01:00 – 04:07 = **~3h07m**
- Last ANY-mode departure: ~01:00-01:07 (tram 15 or last SBB train)
- First ANY-mode departure: ~04:07 (first SBB train) or ~04:26 (first tram 15)
- The TPG Dec 2025 extension to 00:30+ on all lines likely pushes last bus/tram service to ~00:30-01:00

**Hard dead window (zero public transit):** approximately **01:10 to 04:07 = ~2h57m**

### Institutional Transport

- **HUG (Hopitaux Universitaires de Geneve):** No publicly documented employee shuttle or night transport service found. HUG's main campus (Rue Gabrielle-Perret-Gentil 4) is ~3.5 km from Lancy-Pont-Rouge by road. The closest Leman Express station to HUG is **Champel-Hopital** (underground pedestrian tunnel to HUG), not Lancy-Pont-Rouge. Night staff at HUG likely rely on personal vehicles or on-site rest rooms during the dead window.
- **Employer shuttles:** No documented employer shuttles serving Lancy-Pont-Rouge during dead window hours.
- **Aerobus (TPG):** Lines A1-A6 operate 03:00-06:00 serving Geneva Airport — Line A1 passes through Carouge (near Lancy-Pont-Rouge area) but is designed for airport passengers (requires boarding pass), not general commuters.
- **Cross-border bus (France):** No weekday night cross-border bus service found. Noctambus route 60 (Gex/Ferney) is Fri/Sat only.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

**The core insight: Lancy-Pont-Rouge at 02:00 is largely EMPTY of cross-border workers.**

The ~160,000 frontaliers mostly work standard daytime hours and cross back to France by 18:00-19:00. The Bardonnex/Perly border crossings are open 24/7, but night traffic is minimal (daytime congestion peaks at 17:00). The customs *office* at Bardonnex operates Mon-Fri 07:00-17:00 only.

**People physically present at 02:00:**
1. **Pont-Rouge quartier residents** (~1,800 people in 640 housing units): sleeping in the new residential development opened 2020. These are Swiss/permanent residents, not frontaliers.
2. **HUG night staff who are frontaliers** (~1,900-2,400 nurses/staff from France): they are at HUG (3.5 km away), NOT at Lancy-Pont-Rouge. 60% of HUG nurses are French frontaliers who cannot return home during a night shift. They are stranded AT THE HOSPITAL, not at the train station.
3. **Security/maintenance workers** in commercial buildings around Etoile: small numbers, likely arrived by car.
4. **Late-night travelers** stranded after last train: occasional, not a regular population.

**The frontalier problem is different here:** It is not about being stranded at 02:00 (they are either home in France or locked into a hospital shift). It is about the 05:00-06:00 shift change — nurses finishing night shifts who need to get back to France, and morning shift workers arriving from France. The dead window problem hits the TRANSITION, not the deep night.

### Key Finding

**Lancy-Pont-Rouge is NOT a dead-window stranding problem — it is a shift-change bottleneck.** The 160,000 frontaliers are home in France by nightfall. The people who ARE here at 02:00 (HUG night nurses) are 3.5 km away at the hospital, not at the station. The real gap hits at ~05:00-06:00 when night shift ends and frontaliers need to cross back, or at ~22:00-01:00 when the last cross-border transit runs out and night-shift workers must commit to staying until morning. A "Border Lock" should focus on the TRANSITION moments, not the dead-window middle.

### Dead Window Duration

**Rail dead window at Geneve-Aeroport:** 00:19 – 04:58 = **4h39m** (HIGH confidence, from CSV)

**All-mode dead window (public transit):** ~00:24 – 03:00 = **~2h36m**
- Last ANY-mode public departure: ~00:24 (Bus 57 from Aeroport to ZIMEYSA)
- First ANY-mode public departure: 03:00 (Aerobus A1-A6, airport-bound, requires boarding pass)
- First general public transit: ~04:32 (Bus 57)

**True public dead window (no boarding pass required):** ~00:24 – 04:32 = **~4h08m**

**Critical nuance:** The Aerobus (03:00-06:00) requires an airline ticket/boarding pass. It is NOT available to airport ground staff, logistics workers, or general commuters. For workers, the dead window is the full ~00:24 to ~04:32.

**If RL5 night service to ZIMEYSA confirmed (~02:44):** Dead window could shrink to ~02:44-04:32 = **~1h48m** for ZIMEYSA zone only. This needs verification.

### Institutional Transport

- **Geneva Airport NPA Shuttles:** The airport mobility plan (mobilite.gva.ch) documents internal NPA (Navette Personnel Aeroport) shuttles serving employees with irregular schedules. ~40,000 passengers/year (2014 data, +38% since 2009, likely higher now). These serve employees on the airport platform only — coverage of ZIMEYSA industrial zone unclear. Operating window documented as covering 4am-1am work schedules; **gap at 01:00-04:00 for airport employees** appears likely.
- **Swissport/dnata ground handling:** ~1,500 combined employees at GVA. No publicly documented dedicated night transport — employees likely rely on personal vehicles or NPA shuttles during covered hours.
- **ZIMEYSA zone:** No dedicated employer shuttle found for the industrial zone. Bus 57 (TPG) is the primary public link. RUBIX development (47,000 m2 logistics/industrial) will add workers but no transport plan documented.
- **Hotel shuttles (GVA):** Several airport hotels operate 24h shuttle services, but these serve hotel guests only, not workers.
- **Parking:** Airport restricts employee parking (strict attribution since 2007, declining share). 86% of airport employees work irregular hours (4am-1am range). Many presumably drive and park at P+R facilities or employee lots.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

**This is the one Geneva node where people ARE physically present and working at 02:00.**

**People on-site at 02:00:**

1. **Airport operations staff** (~200-400 estimated night shift):
   - Ground handlers (Swissport, dnata): baggage, ramp operations for early-morning flights (pre-positioning starts ~03:00-04:00)
   - Security (Securitas, police): 24/7 airport security perimeter
   - Cleaning crews: terminal and aircraft cleaning overnight
   - Air traffic control (Skyguide): 24/7 operations
   - Airline duty officers: skeleton crew monitoring
   - Airport infrastructure/maintenance: runway, systems
   - Location: ON the airport platform, inside security perimeter

2. **Logistics/warehouse workers in ZIMEYSA** (~50-150 estimated):
   - Pharma distribution: temperature-controlled warehouse operations (Galenica, Voigt, others)
   - Freight forwarding: cargo processing for early-morning flights
   - E-commerce fulfillment: some 24h operations
   - Swiss Post/Innight operations: medical deliveries staging
   - Location: scattered across ZIMEYSA warehouses, 2-3 km from airport terminal

3. **Truck drivers** (variable):
   - Long-haul drivers resting at or near the freight zone
   - Innight delivery drivers staging for pre-dawn hospital runs
   - Location: truck parking areas along Route de Meyrin, freight zone

4. **Airport hotel guests/staff** (~50-100):
   - NH Geneva Airport, Crowne Plaza, etc.
   - Location: within airport perimeter or adjacent

**How did they get here?**
- Airport platform workers: primarily personal cars (parking lots), some via NPA shuttle (if shift started before 01:00). Those arriving for very early shifts (03:00-04:00) may use Aerobus if they have a boarding pass, otherwise drive.
- ZIMEYSA workers: almost exclusively personal cars. Bus 57 stops at 00:24; no alternative.
- Truck drivers: already in their vehicles.

### Key Finding

**Geneva Airport has a REAL dead-window problem, but it is masked by car dependency.** The 4h39m rail gap and ~4h08m bus gap are among the longest on the corridor. Unlike most nodes, people ARE working here at 02:00 — airport operations never stop. But the transit system treats it as if they do. The airport's NPA shuttle partially covers employees, but ZIMEYSA logistics workers (2-3 km away) have NO institutional transport and rely entirely on cars. The Aerobus (03:00-06:00) only serves airline passengers with boarding passes, creating an ironic situation where transit exists during the dead window but is restricted to the wrong population. A "Cargo Lock" must address the split geography: airport platform (inside security) vs. ZIMEYSA industrial zone (outside, across highway/rail infrastructure).

## Cross-Node Observations

### 1. Geneva's Shannon Diversity (1.95) Masks the Night Collapse

Geneva has 7 transport modes and the highest modal diversity on the corridor. But this diversity is a DAYTIME phenomenon. At night (Mon-Thu), it collapses to effectively 1-2 modes:
- **00:00-00:30:** Rail + bus (operating normally)
- **00:30-01:07:** Rail only (last trains trickling out)
- **01:07-03:00:** NOTHING (hard dead window)
- **03:00-04:07:** Aerobus only (restricted to airline passengers with boarding pass)
- **04:07-05:00:** Rail resuming

The Dec 2025 TPG extension (all lines past 00:30) helps narrow the entry to the dead window but does NOT address the core 01:07-04:07 gap. Shannon diversity at 02:00 on a weekday: effectively **0.0**.

### 2. The TPG "Jeudredis" Innovation (Summer 2026)

From April 30 to September 24, 2026, TPG will extend night service on Thursday-Sunday nights until 03:30. This is branded "jeudredis" (Thursday-Friday). This would nearly CLOSE the dead window on Thursdays in summer — but:
- Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday: still fully dead 01:07-04:07
- October-April: still fully dead every weeknight
- This is a leisure-oriented measure, not a worker-oriented one

### 3. The Frontalier Paradox

Geneva's 160,000+ cross-border workers create the APPEARANCE of a massive night transit need, but the reality is:
- Most frontaliers work 08:00-18:00 and are home in France by evening
- The ~3,200 French nurses at HUG who work night shifts are LOCKED IN at the hospital — they cannot leave during their shift and do not need transit at 02:00
- The real frontalier transit gap is at **shift boundaries**: 21:00-22:00 (arriving for night shift after last cross-border service) and 06:00-07:00 (leaving after night shift before regular service is at full frequency)
- The border crossings at Bardonnex/Perly are physically open 24/7, but without transit, crossing requires a car

### 4. Car Dependency Is the Night-Time Default

Both nodes reveal the same pattern: Geneva's extensive public transit network simply STOPS at night on weekdays, and the population that needs to move (hospital workers, airport staff, logistics workers) defaults to private cars. This is not a failure of the transit system per se — it is a design choice that treats the night as non-existent for public mobility.

For a "Cargo Lock" or "Border Lock," the question is not "how do we fill the transit gap?" but "what is the experience of the person who DOES NOT HAVE A CAR at 02:00?"

### 5. The Two Geneva Night Populations

| Population | At 02:00 | Location | Transit need | Current solution |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| HUG night nurses (frontaliers) | At work | Hospital wards, 3.5 km from Lancy-PR | None during shift; critical at shift end (05:00-06:00) | Stay at hospital; car; colleague ride |
| Airport ground staff | At work | Airport platform (inside security) | None during shift; critical at shift start/end | NPA shuttle (limited); car; colleague ride |
| ZIMEYSA logistics workers | At work | Warehouses, 2-3 km from airport | None during shift; critical at shift change | Car only (no institutional transport) |
| Truck drivers | In cab | Freight zone, Route de Meyrin | None (self-contained) | Truck cab |
| Pont-Rouge residents | Sleeping | Apartments | None | N/A |

### 6. Recommended Siting Implications

- **Node 1 (Lancy-Pont-Rouge):** The "Border Lock" concept needs reframing. The lock should not be at the station (empty at 02:00) but should address the **temporal boundary** when transit runs out and frontaliers must commit to staying overnight in Geneva. Consider siting near HUG or Champel-Hopital station, where the stranded population actually IS.
- **Node 2 (Airport/ZIMEYSA):** The "Cargo Lock" has a clearer spatial logic. The split between airport platform and ZIMEYSA zone creates a 2-3 km gap with no pedestrian infrastructure. The lock could address the **in-between space** — the no-man's-land between airport security perimeter and industrial warehouses where workers cross at shift changes with no transit support.
