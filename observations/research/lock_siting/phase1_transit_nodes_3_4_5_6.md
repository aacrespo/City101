## Node 3 — Nyon-Genolier — Altitude Lock

### Last/First Departures (Weekday) by Mode

| Mode | Operator | Last Departure | First Departure | Dead Window |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Rail (mainline)** | SBB/CFF | 01:05 (Nyon) | 04:23 (Nyon) | 3h18m |
| **Narrow gauge to Genolier** | NStCM R55 | ~21:29 (last train serving Genolier) | ~05:37 (first from St-Cergue) / 06:14 (new 2026: Genolier→Nyon) | **~8h08m to Genolier** |
| **Bus (regional)** | TPN | ~20:00-21:00 (estimated) | ~06:00 (estimated) | ~9h |
| **Taxi** | Local operators | 24/7 (on-demand) | — | None (but CHF 35-45/trip) |

### Dead Window Duration

**Rail (Nyon mainline): 3h18m** (01:05–04:23)

**NStCM to Genolier: ~8h08m** (21:29–05:37). This is the critical gap. The last NStCM train serving Genolier departs Nyon at approximately 21:29. The first morning service from St-Cergue direction arrives ~05:37. From 2026, a new direct Genolier→Nyon train departs 06:14, arriving 06:28.

**Effective dead window for Genolier Clinic staff: ~8 hours.** Night shift workers arriving or departing between 21:30 and 05:30 have ZERO public transit to/from the hilltop clinic.

### Institutional Transport

- **Clinique de Genolier shuttle:** No publicly documented shuttle service found. The clinic (Swiss Medical Network) has ~358 employees and ~202 affiliated doctors. Job listings mention "cadre de travail attractif" but no specific transport benefit. Staff are expected to drive — the clinic has ample hilltop parking.
- **Taxi service:** Available 24/7 from Nyon. Estimated CHF 35-45 for Nyon→Genolier (~7 min drive, ~6 km). Night surcharge likely applies. Multiple operators: Taxi Rafael, Taxi ABC, Nyon Taxis.
- **2026 station rename:** NStCM is renaming "La Joy-Clinique" to "Genolier clinique/campus" — acknowledging the clinic as a destination, but this doesn't extend operating hours.

### Walking Assessment

- **From Genolier Clinic to Nyon station:** ~6.2 km, ~200m elevation drop
- **Terrain:** Steep descent through village roads, then cantonal road. No continuous sidewalk on portions of the route. Narrow, winding road through forested sections.
- **Walking time:** ~75-90 minutes downhill (longer uphill)
- **Safety at 02:00:** Poor. Unlit rural roads between Genolier village and Nyon. No pedestrian infrastructure on the descent. Forested, isolated sections. Not a realistic option.
- **Realistic?** NO. The altitude difference (200m), distance (6+ km), lack of lighting, and absence of pedestrian infrastructure make this dangerous and impractical at night.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

- **Clinique de Genolier night staff:** ~150 workers (nurses, care staff, security, cleaning, kitchen). The clinic has ~100 beds and operates 24/7 including an emergency department.
- **Patients:** ~60-80 inpatients on any given night
- **Location:** Hilltop campus at Route du Muids 3, Genolier (elevation ~550m). Completely isolated from transit. The P+R at Genolier NStCM station is ~1 km from the clinic.
- **GHOL Nyon staff:** ~80-120 night workers at the Nyon site of Groupement Hospitalier de l'Ouest Lémanique, closer to the town center.

### Key Finding

**The NStCM narrow gauge is the bottleneck.** While mainline rail at Nyon runs until 01:05, the connection up to Genolier stops at ~21:29 — creating an 8-hour dead window to the hilltop clinic. An estimated 150 night workers must drive or take taxis (CHF 35-45/trip). The 200m altitude difference makes walking impossible. This is an "altitude lock" in the purest sense: elevation creates total transit dependency on a line that shuts down 4 hours before the mainline corridor.

## Node 4 — Morges — Temporal Lock

### Last/First Departures (Weekday) by Mode

| Mode | Operator | Last Departure | First Departure | Dead Window |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Rail (mainline)** | SBB/CFF | 01:07 (Morges) | 04:01 (Morges) | 2h54m |
| **Bus 701** | MBC | ~00:25 (last from Lausanne direction) | ~05:10 (first weekday) | ~4h45m |
| **Bus 704 (serves hospital)** | MBC | ~00:07 (from Morges, hôpital) | ~05:30 (estimated) | ~5h23m |
| **Bus 702** | MBC | ~23:46 (estimated last) | ~05:45 (estimated) | ~6h |

### Dead Window Duration

**Rail: 2h54m** (01:07–04:01) — one of the shortest on the corridor.

**Bus 704 (hospital route): ~5h23m** (00:07–~05:30). The 704 bus serves Morges, hôpital directly. Last departures from the hospital stop in the evening run approximately every 30 min: 21:07, 21:37, 22:07, 22:37, 23:07, 23:37, 00:07.

**Effective total dead window (all modes): ~3h54m** (from last bus 704 at 00:07 to first rail at 04:01). However, the hospital-to-station connection dies at 00:07, meaning staff finishing night shifts after midnight must walk.

### Institutional Transport

- **EHC Morges shuttle:** No publicly documented shuttle service. EHC's mobility plan (documented on Mobilservice) focuses on: financial assistance for public transit, cycling fleet, parking management, and an electric vehicle for inter-site transport. No night shuttle mentioned.
- **EHC parking:** The hospital offers specific "night and weekend" parking subscriptions for staff, implying car dependency is the norm for night workers.
- **PubliBike:** A bike-sharing station at the hospital is planned but not yet operational. Even if available, not useful at 02:00 in winter.
- **EHC mobility context:** A "large portion of EHC employees work in healthcare with irregular schedules" and "a significant portion are cross-border workers with virtually non-existent public transportation service" (Mobilservice).

### Walking Assessment

- **From EHC Morges to Gare de Morges:** ~1.25 km via town center route (Hospital → Av. Muret south → town center → Rue de la Gare → station)
- **NOT via Avenue de Marcelin** (which goes north, away from the station, toward the Marcelin school complex)
- **Terrain:** Gentle descent. Hospital sits at ~395m, station at ~381.5m. Approximately 13m elevation difference over 1.25 km — essentially flat.
- **Walking time:** 15-20 minutes at normal pace
- **Safety at 02:00:** MODERATE. Town center of Morges is reasonably well-lit. Rue de la Gare is a main commercial street. However, at 02:00 the streets are completely deserted. Some sections through residential areas have limited lighting.
- **Realistic?** YES, but uncomfortable. A 15-20 minute walk through an empty small town at 02:00 is feasible but isolating, especially for a nurse finishing a 12-hour night shift.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

- **EHC Morges night staff:** ~100-150 workers (nurses, doctors, security, cleaning, kitchen, emergency department staff)
- **Patients:** EHC Morges is a 130-bed hospital with emergency department operating 24/7
- **Hospital location:** Avenue de la Gottaz, northern edge of Morges. At ~N 1,152,050 in LV95, just beyond the terrain tile's north edge.
- **Other night workers in Morges:** Estimated ~300 additional (hotels, restaurants, logistics, security) — total ~450 night workers in commune

### Key Finding

**Morges has the shortest rail dead window on the mid-corridor (2h54m), but the gap is the "last mile" from hospital to station.** MBC bus 704 serves the hospital until ~00:07 — reasonably late. The real temporal lock is the 3h54m window from 00:07 to 04:01 when the hospital-to-station connection is broken. The walk is short (15-20 min) and flat, but through an empty town at night. EHC's own mobility plan acknowledges staff with "irregular schedules" and "virtually non-existent public transport" — their solution is parking permits, not transit.

## Node 5 — Crissier-Bussigny-Ecublens — Logistics Engine

### Last/First Departures (Weekday) by Mode

| Mode | Operator | Last Departure | First Departure | Dead Window |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Rail (mainline)** | SBB/CFF | 00:55 (Bussigny) | 04:48 (Bussigny) | 3h53m |
| **Bus 18 (Crissier)** | TL | ~23:46 (from Crissier, Timonet) | ~05:30 (estimated) | ~5h44m |
| **Bus 36 (Closalet/Migros)** | TL | ~22:00-23:00 (estimated) | ~06:00 (estimated) | ~7h |
| **Noctambus N4** | TL | Fri/Sat ONLY | — | N/A weekday |

### Dead Window Duration

**Rail (Bussigny): 3h53m** (00:55–04:48). This is significantly longer than nearby Renens (3h32m) or Morges (2h54m).

**Bus to industrial/logistics zones: ~5h44m minimum.** The last TL bus serving Crissier Timonet departs at ~23:46. Distribution center shift changes typically happen at 22:00 or 06:00. Workers arriving for a 22:00 shift could theoretically use the last bus, but workers leaving after midnight have no public transit.

**Effective dead window: ~4h48m** (00:55 rail to 04:48 rail). For distribution zone workers specifically, the gap is longer since buses stop ~1 hour before rail.

### Institutional Transport

- **Employer shuttles:** No publicly documented shuttle services found for Crissier distribution centers. This is a critical data gap.
- **Known major employers in Crissier logistics zone:**
  - **Feldschlösschen** — logistics platform (60% of stock transfers by rail, ~7,700 rail wagons/year). Night operations confirmed (beverages distribution).
  - **DHL Express Lausanne** — located in Crissier. Likely 24/7 operations.
  - **Coop distribution center** — referenced at Crissier/Aclens area.
  - **Migros** — major commercial center but the actual distribution hub is at Suhr (AG), not Crissier. Crissier hosts the retail center.
  - **Planzer Transport** — Swiss logistics company with national presence; Crissier is within their western Switzerland network.
- **Worker transport pattern:** Logistics workers in Switzerland typically drive to distribution centers. Night shift workers at these facilities almost certainly rely on private vehicles. Public transit was never designed to serve industrial zones at night.

### Walking Assessment

- **From Crissier logistics zone (Timonet/Closalet) to Bussigny station:** ~2.5 km
- **From Crissier logistics zone to Renens station:** ~2.0 km
- **Terrain:** Flat to gently undulating. Industrial/commercial zone roads.
- **Walking time:** 25-35 minutes
- **Safety at 02:00:** POOR. Industrial zone roads designed for truck traffic, not pedestrians. Limited sidewalks, poor lighting between warehouses. Large parking lots, loading docks. The Route de Cossonay and Chemin du Closalet have truck traffic even at night.
- **Realistic?** NO for practical purposes. While physically walkable, the environment is hostile to pedestrians — these roads were built for trucks and cars. No one walks here at 02:00.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

- **Distribution/logistics workers:** Unknown exact number. Crissier is identified as a "major distribution hub for western Switzerland." Estimated hundreds of workers across all facilities during night shifts.
- **Feldschlösschen night crew:** Beverage distribution operates through the night to supply western Switzerland retail by morning.
- **DHL sorting/distribution:** Express parcel sorting likely runs 24/7.
- **Security personnel:** Warehouse security across the zone.
- **Truck drivers:** In-transit and loading/unloading at docks.
- **Key distinction:** These workers are AT distribution centers, not at or near train stations. The gap is not temporal — it is spatial. Transit was never designed to reach them.

### Key Finding

**Crissier-Bussigny is fundamentally different from the other nodes.** The gap is not primarily temporal (dead window) — it is spatial and structural. Distribution centers were built for truck access, not transit. Night workers drive because the entire zone is designed around car/truck logistics. Even if rail ran 24/7, the last-mile problem from station to warehouse would remain. This is a "visibility lock" — these workers are invisible to the transit system because they operate in spaces transit was never built to serve. Employer-provided transport (shuttles, van pools) likely exists but is invisible to public data.

## Node 6 — Lausanne CHUV — Gradient Dispatcher

### Last/First Departures (Weekday) by Mode

| Mode | Operator | Last Departure | First Departure | Dead Window |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Rail (mainline)** | SBB/CFF | 00:34 (Lausanne Gare) | 04:38 (Lausanne Gare) | 4h04m |
| **M2 Metro (Mon-Thu)** | TL | ~00:40-00:45 (last from CHUV station, Mon-Thu) | ~05:15-05:43 (first service) | **~4h30m-5h03m** |
| **M2 Metro (Fri-Sat)** | TL | ~01:00 (extended, Fri/Sat only) | ~05:15 | ~4h15m |
| **Bus 8** | TL | ~23:30-00:00 (estimated) | ~05:30 (estimated) | ~5h30m |
| **Bus 25** | TL | ~23:00-23:30 (estimated) | ~06:00 (estimated) | ~6h30m |
| **Lausanne-Flon (M2 terminus)** | TL | 00:36 | 05:40 | **5h04m** |

### Dead Window Duration

**M2 Metro (CRITICAL — Mon-Thu): ~4h30m-5h03m.** The M2 operates Mon-Thu from ~05:15/05:43 to ~00:40/00:45. On Fri/Sat, three additional departures extend service to ~01:00. The M2 does NOT run at 02:00 on any night.

**Rail: 4h04m** (00:34–04:38).

**All modes combined: ~4h00m** (from last M2 at ~00:45 to first rail at ~04:38). During this window, CHUV is completely cut off from mechanized descent to the city center/station.

**The Lausanne-Flon dead window is the longest single-mode gap on the corridor: 5h04m** (00:36–05:40).

### The Gradient Problem

- **CHUV elevation:** ~540m (Rue du Bugnon, on the hillside above city center)
- **Lausanne Gare elevation:** ~450m
- **Ouchy (lakeside) elevation:** ~378m
- **Gradient from CHUV to Gare:** ~90m descent over ~1 km (steep)
- **Gradient from CHUV to Ouchy:** ~162m descent over ~2.5 km (very steep)

The M2 metro exists precisely because of this gradient — it was built to connect the lakeside, station, city center, CHUV, and Epalinges along a 340m elevation range. When the M2 stops, **the gradient itself becomes the barrier.** A nurse finishing a night shift at CHUV at 02:00 must descend 90m of steep hill to reach the station — in the dark, possibly in winter conditions.

### Institutional Transport

- **CHUV mobility plan:** The CHUV has a formal mobility plan offering:
  - 15% subsidy on public transit subscriptions
  - 6 Mobility car-sharing vehicles for professional travel
  - Preferential parking rates for staff
- **CHUV night parking:** "Spécial CHUV" nocturnal rate: CHF 56/month for Mon-Fri 17h-09h + weekends/holidays free. A "14-hour" option at CHF 167/month. Parking authorization criteria include: "frequent early or late shifts" — confirming night workers are expected to drive.
- **CHUV shuttle:** No dedicated night shuttle found. A 24 Heures article references a proposed Ouchy-CHUV shuttle to "decongest the metro" — but this appears to be a daytime peak proposal, not a night service.
- **Night bus:** A night bus from Lausanne Gare to CHUV operates **Thursday, Friday, Saturday only** (Noctambus). Takes ~13 minutes. NOT available Mon-Wed.
- **State of Vaud parking decision:** The Canton has specifically addressed parking for CHUV collaborators, acknowledging the problem of night-shift access.

### Walking Assessment

- **From CHUV to Lausanne Gare:** ~1.0-1.2 km, but 90m steep descent
- **Route:** Rue du Bugnon → Rue du Pont → Place de la Riponne → Rue Centrale → Gare
- **Terrain:** STEEP. Multiple steep streets, stairways (escaliers du marché, etc.). Lausanne is famously built on hills with the Flon gorge running through the center.
- **Walking time:** 15-20 minutes downhill (25-30 minutes uphill)
- **Safety at 02:00:** MODERATE to POOR. City center streets are lit but deserted. Steep, narrow streets can be icy in winter. The Riponne area has some nightlife but is largely empty Mon-Thu nights.
- **Realistic?** BARELY. Physically possible for a fit person in good weather. In winter (ice, rain, 2:00 AM darkness after a 12-hour shift), the steep descent is genuinely hazardous. This is why the M2 was built.

### Who Is Here at 02:00?

- **CHUV night staff:** ~2,500-3,000 workers. CHUV has 12,844 employees total, with ~5,257 nurses across 24h shifts. Night shift includes nurses, doctors, lab technicians, pharmacists, cleaning staff, security, kitchen staff, porters, IT.
- **This is the largest single concentration of night workers on the entire 101km corridor.**
- **UNIL students/researchers:** Some may be at adjacent university buildings (late study, research), though few at 02:00.
- **Patients and visitors:** Emergency department operates 24/7.
- **Location:** CHUV campus, Rue du Bugnon 21, ~540m elevation. Served by M2 metro station "CHUV" — but M2 is not running.

### Key Finding

**The M2 metro does NOT run at 02:00 — confirming that the 250m gradient IS the gap.** On weekdays (Mon-Thu), the M2 stops at approximately 00:45 and resumes at ~05:15-05:43, creating a ~4.5-5 hour dead window. During this time, 2,500-3,000 CHUV night workers are stranded on a steep hillside. The CHUV's own mobility plan tacitly acknowledges this: night parking is subsidized (CHF 56/month), parking authorization criteria explicitly list "frequent early or late shifts," and the Canton of Vaud has specifically addressed CHUV staff parking. The institutional response to the M2 gap is: **drive.** This makes CHUV the single most critical node on the corridor — the largest concentration of night workers, on the steepest gradient, with the longest metro dead window.

## Cross-Node Observations

### 1. The Universal Response to Night Gaps Is: Drive

Across all four nodes, institutions have responded to the transit dead window the same way:
- **Genolier Clinic:** Hilltop parking, no shuttle
- **EHC Morges:** Night parking subscriptions, no shuttle
- **Crissier logistics:** Facilities designed for car/truck access
- **CHUV:** Subsidized night parking (CHF 56/month), Canton-level parking policy

Public transit operators have not addressed the weekday night gap. Noctambus runs Fri/Sat only. The M2 adds 3 extra runs Fri/Sat but nothing Mon-Thu. The institutional assumption is: if you work at night, you have a car.

### 2. Altitude Amplifies the Gap

Two of the four nodes (Genolier and CHUV) involve significant elevation changes that make the transit gap physically impassable on foot:
- **Genolier:** 200m above Nyon, 6+ km, no sidewalks — impossible to walk
- **CHUV:** 90m above Gare, steep city streets — hazardous to walk, especially in winter

The altitude dimension transforms a temporal gap (no service for X hours) into a spatial barrier (you physically cannot get there).

### 3. The Dead Window Gets Wider as You Move from Station to Institution

| Node | Rail Dead Window | Last-Mile Dead Window | Amplification |
|------|------------------|-----------------------|---------------|
| Nyon (rail) → Genolier (clinic) | 3h18m | **~8h** | 2.4x |
| Morges (rail) → EHC Morges | 2h54m | ~3h54m | 1.3x |
| Bussigny (rail) → Crissier logistics | 3h53m | ~5h44m+ | 1.5x |
| Lausanne (rail) → CHUV | 4h04m | ~4h30m (M2) | 1.1x |

The "last mile" from station to institution consistently extends the dead window, sometimes dramatically (Genolier: 2.4x amplification).

### 6. Hierarchy of Severity

1. **CHUV (Node 6)** — Most critical. 2,500-3,000 workers, steep gradient, longest metro gap (5h04m at Flon). Institutional response is subsidized car parking.
2. **Genolier (Node 3)** — Most isolated. 150 workers, 200m altitude barrier, 8-hour dead window to hilltop. Zero public transit alternatives.
3. **Morges (Node 4)** — Most walkable but still broken. 100-150 hospital workers, short flat walk, but dead streets at 02:00.
4. **Crissier (Node 5)** — Structurally different. Workers in car-only infrastructure. The gap is spatial, not just temporal.
