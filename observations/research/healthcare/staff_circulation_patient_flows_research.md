## 2. STAFF SHIFT PATTERNS

### Standard Swiss hospital 3x8 shifts

Swiss public hospitals (CHUV, HUG) operate on a rotating 3x8 model:

| Shift | CHUV typical hours | HUG typical hours |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Early (matin) | 07:00–15:24 | 07:00–15:30 |
| Late (après-midi) | 14:44–23:06 | 13:15–21:45 |
| Night (nuit) | 22:45–07:15 | 21:15–07:15 |

Key observations:
- Shifts include weekends, nights, and public holidays
- Overlap periods exist between shifts for handover (about 15-30 min)
- Night shift change happens around **22:45-23:00** (start) and **07:00-07:15** (end)
- Full-time nurses work approximately 6 night shifts per month

[SOURCE: Infirmiers.com forum — https://www.infirmiers.com/forum/suisse-planning-en-3x8-t84326.html]
[SOURCE: Forum Welcome Suisse — https://forum.welcome-suisse.ch/t/horaires-de-travail-pour-infirmiere-en-suisse/10541]

### Night work in Switzerland — general statistics
- Approximately 4-5% of Swiss workers work regularly at night (23:00-06:00)
- In industrialized countries, 20% of employees are affected by night work (SECO figure)
- OFS: 8% work in shifts, 3% at night, 5% both shift and night
- Night workers in hospitals must undergo regular medical examinations
- Employers must provide 10% compensatory time off for night hours (for 25+ nights/year)

[SOURCE: SECO — https://www.seco.admin.ch/seco/fr/home/Arbeit/Arbeitsbedingungen/Arbeitnehmerschutz/Arbeits-und-Ruhezeiten/Nacht-und-Sonntagsarbeit.html]
[SOURCE: Santé Romande — https://santeromande.ch/travail-de-nuit-travail-qui-nuit-a-la-sante/]

## 3. CROSS-BORDER WORKERS (FRONTALIERS) — THE STRANDED NURSES

### Scale of the phenomenon
- 17.6% of healthcare workers in the franco-valdo-genevois region are cross-border workers = **7,200 people** out of 41,000 total
- At HUG specifically: **60% of nursing staff live in Haute-Savoie or the Ain**
- Of 2,352 HUG nurses: 87% resided in Haute-Savoie, 4.6% in the Ain
- 404 cross-border aides-soignants also counted
- Healthcare represents 13% of all cross-border workers in Geneva — the single largest sector
- Growth: +175% cross-border health workers in canton Vaud over 10 years; +65% in Geneva

[SOURCE: Groupe Ecomedia — https://groupe-ecomedia.com/sante-moins-de-personnels-frontaliers-a-lhopital-de-geneve/]
[SOURCE: Amicale des Frontaliers — https://www.amicale-frontaliers.org/blog/geneve-ne-fera-plus-appel-au-personnel-de-sante-frontalier]
[SOURCE: Le Temps — https://www.letemps.ch/suisse/geneve/suisse-dependante-frontaliers]

### Salary differential driving the flow
- Nurse salary at HUG is on average **2.5x higher** than in neighboring France
- HUG and French authorities signed agreement (October 2025) to slow massive recruitment of French healthcare workers by Swiss establishments

[SOURCE: CA Frontaliers — https://www.ca-frontaliers.com/magazine/dossiers/hopitaux-universitaires-de-geneve/]
[SOURCE: Leman Bleu — https://www.lemanbleu.ch/fr/Actualites/Geneve/Les-HUG-limitent-leur-recours-au-personnel-infirmier-francais.html]

### The night stranding problem

**Léman Express (cross-border rail):**
- L5 line: trains every 30 min from 05:00 to 00:30 daily
- Weekend nights (Fri-Sat, Sat-Sun): additional hourly trains 01:00-05:00
- L4: last departure from Annemasse at 23:50 (arrives Coppet 00:41)
- **Monday through Thursday nights: NO service between ~00:30 and ~05:00**

This means: a French nurse finishing the late shift at HUG at 23:06 can catch the last Léman Express. But a nurse finishing a night shift change at 23:00 at GHOL Nyon? There is no cross-border train from Nyon at all — the Léman Express runs Geneva-Annemasse only.

[SOURCE: Léman Express — https://www.lemanexpress.com/en/2025/12/09/horaire-2026/]

### What happens to frontalier night workers?
- They must drive. No cross-border night public transport on weekdays.
- HUG night parking subscription: CHF 75/month (H-Nord or H-Cluse)
- Parking access: 17:00-08:00 weekdays, 24h weekends/holidays
- HUG subsidizes public transport subscriptions (but irrelevant at night)
- Allocation criterion: if you live >60 min by public transport or >30 min walk, you qualify for parking

[SOURCE: Fondation des Parkings HUG — https://www.geneve-parking.ch/fr/parkings-des-hug]
[SOURCE: HUG MobilHUG — https://www.hug.ch/mobilhug/voitures]
[SOURCE: 20 Minutes — https://www.20min.ch/fr/story/parquer-aux-hug-le-calvaire-des-employes-157721352834]

### Key gap: No hospital shuttle services found
- Despite extensive searching, **no evidence of dedicated night shuttle services for hospital staff** was found at any corridor hospital
- HUG's mobility plan focuses on public transport subsidies and parking allocation, not shuttles

### Night bus services — fragmented and weekend-only

**Geneva — Noctambus:**
- Operates ONLY Friday-Saturday and Saturday-Sunday nights (00:00-05:00)
- 12 urban lines covering 68 communes (45 Geneva, 13 Vaud, 10 French)
- **NOT available Monday-Thursday nights** — the very nights when healthcare workers need them
- Recently rebranded into tpg night network with same weekend-only limitation

[SOURCE: TPG — https://www.tpg.ch/en/travel/more-lines/night-service]
[SOURCE: Noctambus — https://noctambus.ch/horaires-et-plans]

**Lausanne — TL Night Network:**
- Lines N1-N6 running approximately 00:40-05:00
- [Schedule days NOT FOUND — needs verification: likely also weekend-only]

[SOURCE: TL — https://www.t-l.ch/en/travel/timetables/night-network/]

**Nyon/Morges region — CarPostal night buses:**
- Multiple regional lines (10.720 through 10.846)
- Cover Allaman, Aubonne, Rolle, Gland, Nyon corridors
- Surcharge CHF 3.00 per person per journey
- **Service only Friday/Saturday and Saturday/Sunday nights**
- On-demand PubliCar: Fri/Sat and Sat/Sun, 22:30-02:30 only

[SOURCE: CarPostal — https://www.postauto.ch/fr/informations-de-voyage/bus-de-nuit/nyon-et-de-gland-vd]

### Taxi as last resort
- 24/7 taxi services exist (Taxis Dany covers Gland-Aubonne-Nyon-Morges-Geneva-Lausanne)
- Night rates apply 22:00-06:00
- Sample fares from Lausanne station: Morges CHF 55, Nyon CHF 160, Vevey CHF 80
- Per-km rates: Nyon ~CHF 3.43/km, Vevey ~CHF 5.37/km
- **A night nurse in Nyon commuting from Morges by taxi: ~CHF 55 each way = CHF 110/night**

[SOURCE: Allo Taxi — https://www.allotaxi.ch/Vaud.html]
[SOURCE: Taxis Dany — https://www.taxis-dany.ch/taxi/]
[SOURCE: Taxis Nyon — https://www.taxis-nyon.com/pages/tarifs-taxi-nyon]

### The person who falls through the cracks

**Scenario 1: Night nurse at GHOL Nyon, lives in Gland**
- Shift ends 07:15. First NStCM/bus: ~05:30-06:00. She's fine in the morning.
- Shift starts 22:45. Last regional bus to Nyon from Gland: early evening. She drives.
- No Noctambus coverage (Noctambus = Geneva canton only, Nyon is Vaud)
- CarPostal night bus: weekends only
- **Monday-Thursday: car is the only option**

**Scenario 2: Frontalier nurse at HUG, lives in Annemasse**
- Late shift ends 23:06. Last Léman Express L5 at ~00:30. She can make it — barely.
- Night shift starts 21:15. She takes the train, fine.
- **But if she misses the 00:30 train: stranded until 05:00. A taxi to Annemasse: ~CHF 40-60.**

**Scenario 3: Night aide-soignant at EHC Morges, lives in Aubonne (village)**
- No night bus Monday-Thursday
- CarPostal night bus only on weekends (line 10.721 Rolle-Aubonne-Allaman)
- **Must own a car to work night shifts. Period.**

[SOURCE: Citypopulation — https://www.citypopulation.de/en/switzerland/admin/vaud/B2228__district_de_nyon/]

### The gaps in coverage
- **Between Nyon (km 25) and Morges (km 48): 23 km with no hospital.** This stretch passes through Gland, Rolle, Aubonne — towns covered by EHC satellite sites and GHOL Rolle outpost, but no emergency department between Nyon and Morges.
- **Between Morges (km 48) and CHUV (km 65): 17 km** but well-served via Crissier/Bussigny suburbs, which are functionally Lausanne.
- **Between CHUV (km 65) and HRC Rennaz (km 89): 24 km.** The old Vevey hospital provided intermediate care, but HRC consolidation moved acute care to Rennaz. HRC Providence in Vevey retains geriatric/rehabilitation only.

### CHUV as tertiary referral hub

- **>10,000 patient transfers per year** from/to CHUV
- Reaches 1,500+ different destinations
- 100-150 international repatriations annually
- ~100 helicopter flights per year
- 98% of transfers go to/from hospitals within 75 km radius
- Most transferred patients: internal medicine, chronic, elderly (79% over age 65)
- Psychiatric transfers have grown significantly: 24% of ambulance transfers
- Transport modes: ambulance (most frequent), adapted seated transport, helicopter (rare)

[SOURCE: CHUV coordination transferts — https://www.chuv.ch/fr/dso/dso-home/organisation-vision/transfert-patients]
[SOURCE: Cairn.info study — https://shs.cairn.info/revue-sante-publique-2013-1-page-51?lang=fr]

### CHUV specialties driving referral transfers
Patients are transferred to CHUV for: cardiothoracic surgery, neurosurgery, major trauma, transplant, oncology, burns, neonatal intensive care, and other ultra-specialized services not available at regional hospitals.

Regional hospitals (GHOL, EHC, HRC) handle: general medicine, general surgery, orthopedics, obstetrics (basic), emergency (stabilization before transfer if needed).

[SOURCE: CHUV — https://www.chuv.ch/fr/chuv-home]

### Rennaz — The "Hospital Island" problem

The HRC Rennaz site (opened autumn 2019) has been widely criticized for poor public transport accessibility:

- Located in a small village with poor communication routes
- 2 km from Villeneuve station — no direct rail connection
- Bus VMCV 201 runs every 20 min (daytime), but frequency drops in evenings
- From Chablais valaisan: Saint-Gingolph to Rennaz = **44 min by public transport vs 16 min by car** (2.75x longer)
- Port-Valais (Bouveret) to Rennaz: **36 min by public transport vs 13 min by car**
- A proposed monorail/hectometric transport connecting to CFF Simplon line was studied but never built
- The bus line costs burden fell on communes of Rennaz and Noville
- Still debated: creating a railway halt on the Simplon line near the hospital

## 8. SYNTHESIS — THE HEALTHCARE CORRIDOR AS SPATIAL SYSTEM

### Key numbers summary

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total hospital employees along corridor | ~32,000-33,000 |
| Estimated night shift workers | 4,600-6,300 |
| Cross-border healthcare workers (frontaliers) | 7,200 in franco-valdo-genevois region |
| % of HUG nurses living in France | 60% |
| CHUV inter-hospital transfers per year | >10,000 |
| Night transport dead window | ~00:30-05:00 (4.5 hours) |
| Night bus availability (weekdays) | ZERO on the entire corridor |
| HRC Rennaz catchment | 180,000 people |
| HRC Rennaz — distance to nearest station | 2 km |
| Genolier last train | ~21:29 |
| Patient hotel rooms (CHUV) | 108 |
| Ronald McDonald rooms (HUG) | 10 |

### What the corridor reveals

1. **The corridor is a labor pipeline.** Over 30,000 healthcare workers commute daily, with the heaviest flows toward the two poles (HUG and CHUV). The middle section (Nyon-Morges) has smaller hospitals but still employs 3,000+ people who must navigate the same dead window.

2. **Cross-border dependency is structural.** 60% of HUG's nurses live in France. The Léman Express partially addresses this for weekday evenings, but the dead window (00:30-05:00) on weekdays forces car dependency. Weekend night trains exist but are hourly.

3. **Night transport is designed for leisure, not labor.** Noctambus, NightBird, and CarPostal night buses all operate Friday-Saturday and Saturday-Sunday only. No night bus runs Monday through Thursday on any part of the corridor. Healthcare never stops, but transit does.

4. **The car is the night shift's lifeline.** HUG charges CHF 75/month for night parking. EHC Morges and GHOL Nyon have hospital parking but no specific night programs found. A nurse without a car cannot work night shifts at any hospital between Nyon and Rennaz on weekdays.

5. **Rennaz is architecturally disconnected.** Built to consolidate Riviera-Chablais care into one modern facility, it was placed 2 km from the nearest rail station in a village with minimal transit. The result: a 180,000-person catchment area that is 2.75x slower to reach by public transport than by car.

6. **Transitional spaces cluster at the poles.** HUG has Ronald McDonald Houses; CHUV has the Hôtel des Patients (108 rooms) and Maison d'Intervalle. The five hospitals between them — GHOL, EHC, Hirslanden, La Source, HRC — have no equivalent patient/family accommodation found. Families of patients at regional hospitals must find their own lodging.

7. **The gap between Nyon and Morges (23 km) is the corridor's healthcare desert.** No emergency department, no hospital, just EHC satellite clinics and GHOL Rolle outpost (limited services). Someone having a cardiac event in Gland or Rolle at 02:00 depends entirely on ambulance response time.
