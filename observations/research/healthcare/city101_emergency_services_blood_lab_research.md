## 1. AMBULANCE / EMERGENCY SERVICES

### 1.1 Dispatch System (144)

The 144 emergency number connects to cantonal dispatch centers (CASU - Centre d'Appels Sanitaires Urgents):

- **Canton de Vaud**: CASU based at CHUV Lausanne. Formerly "Fondation Urgences Sante," became an autonomous CHUV service as of July 1, 2024. Dispatches approximately 63,000 missions per year. [SOURCE: https://www.chuv.ch/fr/144/]
- **Canton de Geneve**: CASU 144 at HUG manages all ambulance movements across the Geneva canton, including non-urgent transports and inter-hospital transfers. [SOURCE: https://www.hug.ch/urgences/centrale-144]
- **Key difference**: Each canton operates its own independent CASU. Regulators must be paramedics (ES) or nurses (ES) with 1,040 hours of training at ES ASUR Lausanne. [SOURCE: https://www.ambulancier-lesite.fr/systemes-secours-suisse-144/]

### 1.2 National Response Time Standard

- **IVR 90-15 rule**: 90% of all emergencies must be reached within 15 minutes, as recommended by the Interverband fur Rettungswesen (IVR). [SOURCE: https://www.144.ch/ and https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9316220/]
- Basel City achieved this in 96% of cases in 2018 (as benchmark). [SOURCE: https://theparamediclogbook.com/?p=768]
- [NOT FOUND - needs verification] Specific compliance rates for Vaud and Geneva cantons along the corridor.

### 1.6 Coverage Gaps (Analysis)

**Critical gap areas along the corridor:**

1. **Gland-Rolle stretch** (~15 km between Nyon SMUR western limit at Rolle and EHC Morges eastern limit): Covered by CSU Morges-Aubonne from Aubonne base, but distances are significant. SMUR coverage boundary sits roughly at Rolle.

2. **Lavaux section (Lausanne-Vevey, ~20 km)**: UNESCO-protected terraced vineyards with narrow, winding roads, steep staircases, and limited vehicle access. The main road (Route de Lavaux) is constrained. No ambulance base between Lausanne and La Tour-de-Peilz. [ESTIMATED response time: 20-25 min from either direction for vineyard communities like Cully, Rivaz, Epesses]

3. **Night reduction**: With SIS Geneva reducing from 3 to 1 caserne, Riviera going from 3-4 to 2 crews, and Lausanne's ongoing staffing challenges, the 01:00-05:00 window has the thinnest coverage.

## 2. REGA (Air Rescue)

### 2.1 Bases Serving the Corridor

| Base | Location | Missions/year | Aircraft | Crew |
|------|----------|--------------|----------|------|
| **Rega 4 (Lausanne)** | Lausanne-La Blecherette Airport | ~800 | Airbus H145 D3 (since April 2025) | 3 teams of 3: pilot + paramedic + flight physician, 24h standby |
| **Rega 15 (Geneva)** | Geneva Airport (LSGG) | ~350-400 | Airbus H145 D3 (since June 2025) | Full crew with new crew bedrooms for night standby |

- Combined Romandie bases (Geneva, Lausanne, Sion): **1,622 missions in 2024**. [SOURCE: https://www.swissinfo.ch/eng/various/rega-organized-almost-20000-missions-in-2024/88818312]
- Nationally: ~20,000 total missions in 2024 (6.1% above 5-year average), of which 14,714 by helicopter. [SOURCE: same]
- Mission types from Lausanne: road/sports accidents, interhospital transfer flights, organ transport. Serves at least 6 cantons from this base.

### 2.2 Night Flight Capability

- **Yes, full night operations.** ~25% of Lausanne base missions are at night. [SOURCE: https://www.rega.ch/en/our-missions/locations-and-infrastructure/rega-4-lausanne-base]
- One in four helicopter missions Rega-wide take place at night. Over 30 years of NVG (night vision goggle) experience. [SOURCE: https://www.rega.ch/en/news/news-from-the-world-of-rega/detail/new-night-vision-goggles-for-rega]
- New NVGs since spring 2021 on all helicopters: 25% larger field of view, reduced eye fatigue, EASA-certified. [SOURCE: same]
- Geneva base has new crew bedrooms installed specifically to ensure continuous day/night standby. [SOURCE: https://www.rega.ch/en/our-missions/locations-and-infrastructure/rega-15-base-geneva]
- Equipment for night operations: digital maps, satellite-supported images on cockpit monitors, powerful spotlights for illuminating landing/accident sites.

### 2.3 REGA vs Road Ambulance

- REGA can reach any location in Switzerland within **15 minutes** of alert. [SOURCE: https://www.rega.ch/en/our-missions/locations-and-infrastructure]
- For most lowland missions, road ambulances are equally fast or faster (if roads are clear and ambulance ready within 10 minutes). [SOURCE: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8650228/]
- REGA advantage is strongest in mountainous/remote areas and for the Lavaux section with its winding roads.
- REGA called for: suspected stroke, heart attack, back injury, severe burns, or when swift/gentle transport to a central hospital is needed. Emergency number: **1414**. [SOURCE: https://www.rega.ch/en/our-missions/this-is-how-we-help-you]

### 3.3 Blood Product Logistics

**Transport chain:**
- Blood transfusion centers are responsible for the **entire transport chain** from collection to hospital delivery. [SOURCE: https://www.swissmedic.ch/swissmedic/en/home/ueber-uns/publikationen/visible/swissmedic-visible-nov-2022.spa.v6.app/en/journey-of-blood.html]
- Red blood cells stored at **4 degrees C**, shelf life **42 days**. [SOURCE: same]
- Platelets stored at **20-24 degrees C** (room temperature with agitation).
- Swissmedic licenses required for manufacturing; strict quality assurance and inspection regime. [SOURCE: https://www.swissmedic.ch/swissmedic/en/home/services/documents/blood-and-blood-components.html]

**Night delivery:**
- **Swiss Post "Innight Medica"**: Overnight logistics for 220+ hospitals in Switzerland. Collects consignments, delivers by 06:00 to reception, operating theatre, or other agreed delivery points. Returns collected 17:00-20:00. [SOURCE: https://www.post.ch/en/sending-parcels/express-and-courier/swiss-express-innight-medica]
- **SwissConnect**: Urgent night pickups with overnight delivery throughout Switzerland. Part of a network of 700 couriers and 80 courier companies. [SOURCE: https://swissconnect.ch/en/our-services/delivery-methods/overnight-shipping]
- [NOT FOUND - needs verification] Specific night emergency blood delivery protocol for hospitals along the corridor. Whether taxi, ambulance, or dedicated courier is used for stat blood requests at 03:00 is unclear.

### 3.4 Cold Chain Requirements

- Whole blood/red cells: **1-6 degrees C** during transport [SOURCE: WHO standards via https://www.who.int/teams/health-product-policy-and-standards/standards-and-specifications/blood-and-products-of-human-origin/blood-cold-chain]
- Platelets: **20-24 degrees C** with continuous gentle agitation
- Fresh frozen plasma: **-25 degrees C or below** (frozen)
- Transport in validated isothermal containers with temperature monitoring

## 4. LAB SAMPLE TRANSPORT

### 4.1 Central / Reference Laboratories

**CHUV Department of Laboratory Medicine and Pathology (DMLP):**
- Address: Rue du Bugnon 46, 1011 Lausanne
- Acts as confirmation/referral laboratory for specialized analyses
- Departments: clinical chemistry, hematology, microbiology, genetics, immunology-allergy, pathology
- Reception of external samples available; courier or postal services can deliver to lab reception (not covered by CHUV). [SOURCE: https://www.chuv.ch/fr/laboratoires/dl-home/prestations/preanalytique-et-postanalytique/preanalytique-prelevement-et-transport]

**HUG BATlab (Geneva):**
- New laboratory building opened mid-2015, consolidating 32 analysis laboratories into one location
- **~5 million analyses per year**
- Automated tray conveyor elevator (Gilgen Logistics) on 10 floors for internal distribution
- CAP-reception: open 07:00-19:00 Mon-Fri for external samples brought by transporters
- CAP-tri: receives samples **24h/24** via pneumatic tube and internal HUG transporters
- [SOURCE: https://www.hug.ch/en/batlab, https://www.gilgen.com/en/references/geneva-university-hospital-hug-batlab-ch-geneva.html]

### 4.2 Inter-Hospital Sample Transport

**PLEXUS-Sante (Bussigny):**
- Joint CHUV-HUG external logistics platform since Q1 2019
- 4,100 sqm with automated high-rise storage (58,000 crates capacity)
- Stores hospital supplies (not pharmaceuticals/gases): masks, gowns, syringes, medical devices
- Achieves ~2.4M CHF/year in purchasing economies of scale
- Frees 2,000 sqm within each hospital
- [SOURCE: https://www.swissinfo.ch/fre/le-chuv-et-les-hug-inaugurent-leur-magasin-commun/44679840]
- Note: This is supply logistics, not sample transport -- but demonstrates the inter-hospital logistics infrastructure.

**Sample transport model:**
- Smaller hospitals (GHOL Nyon, EHC Morges, HRC Rennaz) send specialized samples to CHUV/HUG central labs for analyses they cannot perform locally.
- Transport via private courier or postal service; cost not covered by CHUV. [SOURCE: https://www.chuv.ch/fr/laboratoires/dl-home/prestations/preanalytique-et-postanalytique/preanalytique-prelevement-et-transport]
- Swiss standard: internal couriers collect samples daily from doctors/hospitals by car. [SOURCE: https://swissconnect.ch/fr/a-propos/blog/articles-blog/comptabilite-des-livraisons-express-dans-les-laboratoires-medicaux-suisses]

### 4.3 Courier Services for Lab Samples

**SwissConnect** (largest Swiss express courier network):
- 700+ local couriers, 80+ courier companies, 20+ years experience
- In 20 largest cities: bike couriers collect samples
- Remote areas: taxi couriers load samples onto locked SBB train compartments
- Transports Category B biological materials (UN 3373)
- Night transport available; pick up late evening, deliver overnight
- Signature-required handover with email confirmation
- [SOURCE: https://swissconnect.ch/en/our-services/industry-solutions/laboratory-medicine]

**Swiss Post "Innight Medica":**
- Night courier for 220+ hospitals
- Also handles biological laboratory consignments via regular post (separate service)
- [SOURCE: https://www.post.ch/en/sending-parcels/express-and-courier/swiss-express-innight-medica]

**SYNLAB/MEDISYN couriers:**
- Uses own courier service or SwissConnect network
- Bike couriers in cities, trains for long distances, cars in remote areas
- [SOURCE: https://swissconnect.ch/en/why-choose-us/success-stories/synlab-suisse-sa]

## 7. SYNTHESIS: KEY FINDINGS FOR ARCHITECTURE PROJECT

### Infrastructure gaps that create behavior along the corridor:

1. **Night coverage drops dramatically**: SIS Geneva goes from 3 to 1 caserne, Riviera from 3-4 to 2 ambulance crews. Walk-in clinics close. The "dead window" (01:00-05:00) concentrates all medical access on 5 hospital ERs spread across 101 km.

2. **The Lavaux gap**: No ambulance base between Lausanne and La Tour-de-Peilz (~20 km). Winding roads, steep terrain, UNESCO-protected landscape. REGA helicopter may be faster than road ambulance here. This is the most under-served section of the corridor for emergency response.

3. **Blood and lab samples are road-dependent**: All inter-hospital flows (blood products, lab samples, organ transport) depend on road infrastructure. The A1/A9 motorway is the critical artery. SwissConnect uses SBB trains for some lab sample transport. Swiss Post Innight delivers to 220+ hospitals overnight.

4. **Two separate systems, one corridor**: Geneva and Vaud operate independent 144 dispatch centers, independent ambulance systems, independent blood services (CTS at HUG vs TIR at Epalinges). The corridor is administratively split but functionally interconnected (CHUV and HUG share PLEXUS logistics, REGA covers both cantons seamlessly).

5. **Concentration at two poles**: HUG (Geneva, km 0) and CHUV (Lausanne, ~km 60) are the gravity centers. Smaller hospitals (Nyon, Morges, Rennaz) handle routine emergencies but transfer complex cases to these two. The blood system, lab system, SMUR system, and REGA system all radiate from these poles.

6. **Staffing is the real bottleneck**: The 2023-2024 SPSL Lausanne crisis (1/3 of staff absent, reinforcement from private companies) shows that infrastructure exists but human capacity is strained. This is a systemic issue, not unique to Lausanne.
