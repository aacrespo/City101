# Comic Book v3 — Gemini — Nurse Night Shift at Morges Temporal Lock

## Style Overview

**Aesthetic**: Jon McNaught (*Kingdom*, *Dockwood*) — risograph / screen-printed comic. Strict 3-color ink separation. Simple bold linework. Observational detail of mundane everyday objects and architecture. Figures with simple features (not silhouettes). Print texture and ink overlap visible throughout. Many small panels showing accumulated quiet moments.

**The 3-color system** (this is the core of McNaught's look):
- **Dark navy-indigo** — outlines, shadows, dark areas, night sky
- **Dusty steel blue / periwinkle** — mid-tones, buildings, surfaces, clothing
- **Muted pink / mauve / salmon** — warm accent, lit windows, skin, highlighted surfaces

Where colors overlap in print, new tones appear: navy + pink = dusty purple. Blue + pink = lavender. This overprint effect is key.

**Palette shift for our story**:
- **Before (panels 1–4)**: heavy navy + dusty blue, very little pink — cold, lonely
- **Transition (panels 5–6)**: pink starts appearing — the Lock's warmth entering the palette
- **Present/Lock (panels 7–12)**: pink/mauve becomes dominant, navy recedes — warm, held

**Character**: Young woman with simple features, dark hair in a low bun, wearing scrubs (dusty blue) and an oversized jacket. Canvas tote bag. Simple linework face — you can see her expression but it's minimal. She looks tired.

**Panel approach**: Mix of wide architectural shots AND close-ups of small mundane details (hands, objects, surfaces) — like McNaught's bathroom sequence or parking lot pages. The mundane details accumulate meaning.

**Gemini instructions for all panels**: Do not include speech bubbles, white outlines, labels, or bordered text boxes. Any sound words or environmental text should appear as floating text integrated into the scene without outlines or bubbles. Do not add any text that is not explicitly described in the prompt.

### Color consistency
- Panels 1–4: heavy navy-indigo + dusty blue, minimal pink — regenerate if too much pink or too bright
- Panel 5: navy + blue + one pink-mauve glowing door
- Panel 6: pink entering from one side
- Panels 7–12: pink-mauve becomes dominant, navy becomes accent — regenerate if still cold/blue-heavy
- ALL panels: must look like 3-color risograph print on off-white paper, visible ink texture

### 3-color ink reference
- **Navy-indigo**: like Pantone 289 or dark indigo risograph ink
- **Dusty steel blue**: like Pantone 5425 or medium blue risograph ink
- **Muted pink-mauve**: like Pantone 5005 or salmon/pink risograph ink
- Where navy overlaps pink: dusty purple appears
- Where blue overlaps pink: soft lavender appears
- Off-white paper shows through in lightest areas
