Produced `DIVERSITY_NEW_ANGLES.md` covering all three angles from S8.2's open items:

**Angle 1 — Anomalies Deep Dive (Vernier-Blandonnet + Coppet)**
- Full data profiles from crossref CSV + web research on both communes
- Vernier: Shannon 1.55 / Richness 7 / 84 trains/hr — "diversity without programming." Adjacent to hydrocarbon tank farm, big-box commercial zone, highest unemployment in GE. Social base exists but hostile built environment prevents encounter.
- Coppet: Shannon 0.0 / Richness 21 / 9.5 trains/hr — "programming without diversity." Historic bourg, 36% foreign but skewing wealthy expat (10.5% English-speaking). Amenities serve homogeneous clientele.
- Comparative table and design brief framing for both failure modes.

**Angle 2 — Frontalier Diversity Perspective**
- Geneva: 114,900 active frontaliers end-2024, record 24,835 new permits. 28% of all Swiss frontaliers.
- Vaud: 45,230 frontaliers (Q2 2024). District of Nyon receives 23%, Lausanne 20%.
- Combined corridor: ~160,000 daily frontalier crossings — larger than Lausanne's population.
- Invisible in ALL diversity metrics (nationality, Shannon, richness, GA pricing).
- Temporal diversity argument: corridor diversity is time-dependent. 160k arrive at 07:00, leave at 18:00. Henna's night city = city without its daytime workforce.
- The 600 who crossed back: former GE residents who moved to France, same job, now statistically invisible.

**Angle 3 — Cuisine-Migration Correlation**
- Vaud 2024 foreign population: French 21%, Portuguese 18%, Italian 10%, Spanish 6%, Kosovar 4%.
- Prediction table for 8 stations mapping expected migration communities to cuisine signals.
- Vernier paradox: most diverse commune in Geneva (47% foreign) but only 1 restaurant in 1000m. Diversity exists, food landscape doesn't.
- Portuguese signal: 53,400 Portuguese in Vaud. They tend Lavaux vineyards but no Portuguese restaurants exist in Lavaux villages. Economically essential, culturally invisible.
