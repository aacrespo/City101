## The Combined Narrative — Why It Works
The linear city's promise is connectivity via the rail spine. But that spine only operates ~20 hours/day and is designed only for commuting. The reality:
- **Daytime**: workers already use trains as offices but the experience is accidental, not designed (no sound isolation, signal drops, crowding)
- **Evening**: time-zone workers for Geneva international orgs need to work late but have nowhere to go
- **Night**: shift workers at hospitals/industry are stranded when trains stop
- **Gap hours (1–5am)**: the city literally disconnects — no rail, minimal bus, the line dies

The prototypology answers all of these with one adaptive system on the existing rail infrastructure.

### THE INDEX
**24-Hour Line Vitality Score** per station/segment:
- Daytime: workspace quality (signal, comfort, amenities, occupancy)
- Evening: late-hour accessibility (venues open, transport frequency, safety)
- Night: gap-hour coverage (distance to anything open, transport alternatives, lighting)
- Combined: population served × hours of service × comfort quality

Segments scoring high across all hours = true linear city
Segments scoring high daytime but zero nighttime = dormitory confirmed
Segments scoring zero across all = intervention priority sites
