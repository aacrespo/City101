# Healthcare Supply Chain — Full System Research
## Geneva–Villeneuve 101km Corridor
### Compiled: 2026-03-15

---

## Overview

This document maps the **complete healthcare supply chain** along the 101km Geneva–Villeneuve rail corridor. It covers 8 flow categories: medication/pharma, food/catering, staff circulation, patient flows, postal/logistics, lab samples/blood, waste/sterile, and emergency/ambulance.

**Key finding**: The corridor functions as a single healthcare system split across two cantons (Geneva and Vaud), anchored by two university hospital poles (HUG at km 0, CHUV at km 65), with an invisible logistics nexus at Bussigny-Crissier (km 58) and critical gaps in the middle sections.

## 1. MEDICATION / PHARMA LOGISTICS

### 1.1 Distribution structure

Switzerland's pharma supply chain: **Manufacturer → Pre-wholesaler (Alloga) → Wholesaler (Galexis/Voigt) → Hospital/Pharmacy**.

**Galexis AG** (Galenica Group) supplies **two-thirds of all Swiss pharmacies**, processing **150,000 order lines/day** and moving **100+ million packs/year**.
[SOURCE: Galenica Network — galenica.com/en/galenica-netzwerk/]

**Galexis Ecublens** (Route de Crochy 2, 1024 Ecublens, ~km 60):
- Second-largest Galexis site, hub for **all French-speaking Switzerland**
- Ships **100,000 packs/day**
- CHF 34M modernization completed 2022
- Hours: Mon-Fri 07:00–19:00, Sat 09:00–17:00
[SOURCE: galenica.com/en/news/entries/2022-09-07_avance_news.php]

**Alloga AG** (Burgdorf, off-corridor, feeds into system):
- Pre-wholesaler; nearly **every second medication pack** in Switzerland passes through Alloga
- Cold chain: 2–8°C down to **-80°C** (mRNA vaccines, biologics)
- Uses **SwissConnect** courier network for express deliveries
[SOURCE: alloga.ch/en/about-alloga/about-alloga.php]

### 1.2 Three pharmacy zones

| Zone | Anchor | Coverage | km |
|------|--------|----------|-----|
| West | HUG Pharmacy | Geneva canton hospitals | 0–20 |
| Central | CHUV Pharmacy | All Vaud health establishments | 20–80 |
| East | PHEL (Pharmacie des Hôpitaux de l'Est Lémanique) | Eastern Vaud + Chablais | 80–101 |

- **HUG Pharmacy**: Supplies all care units across HUG's 8 hospitals. On-call weekends/holidays 09:00–19:00.
[SOURCE: hug.ch/pharmacie]
- **CHUV Pharmacy**: Supplies CHUV **and all health establishments in Vaud**. Produces ~4,000 bags total parenteral nutrition/year.
[SOURCE: chuv.ch/fr/pharmacie/pha-home]
- **PHEL/HRC Rennaz**: Inter-hospital pharmacy since 1984, originally serving 7 hospitals. Global dispensing robot. Moved to HRC Rennaz October 2019.
[SOURCE: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharmacy_of_the_Eastern_Vaud_Hospitals]

### 1.3 Night pharma logistics

- **Swiss Post "Innight Medica"**: Overnight delivery to **220+ hospital operating theaters** by 06:00. Returns collected 17:00–20:00.
[SOURCE: post.ch/en/sending-parcels/express-and-courier/swiss-express-innight-medica]
- **SwissConnect**: Network of **700+ couriers** and **80+ courier companies**. Late evening pickup, overnight delivery, personal handover against signature.
[SOURCE: swissconnect.ch/en/our-services/delivery-methods/overnight-shipping]

### 1.4 Medication shortages

- **700+ medicines currently unavailable** in Switzerland (early 2026)
- Galenica ensures 99% of available medications delivered within one day
- Federal strategy in development: compulsory stocks, early-warning system, domestic production
[SOURCE: galenica.com/en/news/entries/20260113-6399860776-news.php]

## 9. SYNTHESIS: THE CORRIDOR AS HEALTHCARE SYSTEM

### 9.1 The invisible architecture

The healthcare supply chain reveals an architecture invisible to passengers on the train:

1. **The Bussigny-Crissier nexus (Node 4)** is where Geneva and Lausanne's hospital systems physically merge — shared warehouse, shared food production, pharma distribution, medical device manufacturing, all within a few km.

2. **The corridor operates on three time signatures**:
   - **Day (06:00–22:00)**: Full system — trains, buses, all 8 flow categories active
   - **Night (22:00–01:00)**: Reduced — last trains, shift changes, Innight Medica trucks loading
   - **Dead window (01:00–05:00)**: All supply chains 100% road-dependent. Zero public transport. 4,600–6,300 healthcare workers must drive or be stranded.

3. **Two cantons, one system**: Geneva and Vaud run separate 144 dispatch, separate blood banks, separate ambulance systems. But they share PLEXUS logistics, REGA coverage, and patient transfer flows. The administrative border at ~km 20 is invisible to the supply chain.

4. **The gaps define the corridor**: Nyon–Morges (23 km, no ER), Lavaux (20 km, no ambulance base), Rennaz (2 km off-rail, 2.75× slower by transit). These are where the chain breaks.
