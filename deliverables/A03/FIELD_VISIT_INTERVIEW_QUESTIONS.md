# Field Visit — Interview Questions

**City101 · Horizontal Elevator · 7 Healthcare Sites**
**Print this or keep on phone.**

---

## Universal questions (ask at every site)

These work for anyone — staff, patients, visitors, security, passersby.

### Transport & access
1. How did you get here today?
2. Do you know what time the last bus/train passes here?
3. Is there a way to get here (or home from here) after midnight?
4. Do you or people you know rely on a car to get here? Why?

### Night / shift work
5. Do you (or does anyone here) work night shifts?
6. What time do night shifts start and end here?
7. How do night shift workers get home? Car? Wait? Sleep here?
8. Is there anywhere nearby to wait, rest, or eat between shifts?

### The gap
9. Have you ever been stuck here — missed the last train, no ride?
10. What do you do during the gap between the last service and the first?

### The space
11. Is there anywhere around here that feels like it's missing? A place you wish existed?
12. If you could change one thing about getting to/from here, what would it be?

## Site-specific questions

### Site 1 — Geneva North Industrial Belt (Blandonnet/ZIMEYSA)
*Target: security guards, truck drivers, anyone in safety vests, airport workers*

13. What gets delivered here at night? Medical supplies? Packages?
14. Do you know if DHL/Post handles hospital deliveries from here?
15. How many people work here overnight roughly?
16. Is there a break room, canteen, or any shared space for night workers?
17. Where do most night workers come from — Vernier? Meyrin? France?

### Site 2 — Nyon Hospital + Genolier
*Target: hospital reception, nurses, bus drivers, pharmacy staff, Genolier clinic staff*

**At GHOL Nyon (lakeside):**
18. How many staff work the night shift here, approximately?
19. Where do most staff live?
20. Is there a shuttle or transport to Genolier clinic?

**At Genolier (hilltop):**
21. How do staff get up here at night?
22. Is there any bus service after 20:00? After midnight?
23. Do patients' families have trouble visiting — especially without a car?

**At Prangins (if time):**
24. Is there transport for visitors to the psychiatric facility?

### Site 3 — Morges Hospital (EHC)
*Target: hospital staff at entrance/café, station kiosk worker, bus driver*

25. How do night staff get home after their shift ends?
26. What time does the last bus from the hospital leave?
27. Do patients — especially elderly — complain about getting to the hospital?
28. Do you know the walk-in clinics at the station (Permanence des Halles, CMM)? Do people confuse them with the hospital?
29. Has anyone mentioned the 3.0★ Google rating? What frustrates people?

### Site 4 — Crissier-Bussigny Night Belt
*Target: anyone visible outside a warehouse — unlikely, try anyway. Also: Migros delivery drivers, any worker on a smoke break*

30. What's being sorted/shipped from here at night?
31. Does any of this go to hospitals? CHUV? HUG?
32. How many people work here overnight?
33. Is there anywhere to eat, sit, or take a break nearby — within walking distance?
34. How do you get here — car? Is there any other option?

### Site 5 — Lausanne CHUV Perpendicular
*Target: nurses on break, M2 attendant, café workers near staff entrance, anyone walking the gradient*

35. What time does M2 actually stop running? And start in the morning?
36. If you finish a shift at 02:00, how do you get home?
37. Do staff who live uphill (Sallaz, Epalinges, Chailly) have it harder getting home?
38. Is there a difference between where nurses live and where doctors live?
39. Is there any night shuttle or hospital transport for staff?
40. Do patients from the northern heights (Epalinges, Pierre-de-Plan) have trouble getting here?

### Site 6 — Montreux–Glion Altitude Medicine
*Target: funicular operator, Valmont reception, elderly resident, hotel staff*

41. What time does the funicular stop running? When does it start?
42. How do Valmont staff get to work for early/night shifts?
43. Do patients' families visit by car or funicular?
44. Do elderly residents in Glion/Territet have trouble getting to medical appointments?
45. Is there any transport after the funicular stops?

### Site 7 — Rennaz Hospital (HRC)
*Target: hospital reception/info desk, anyone on the Villeneuve→Rennaz road, bus driver, patients in waiting area*

46. How do most people get to this hospital — car, bus, walk?
47. Is there a bus from Villeneuve station? What's the last one?
48. Night shift staff — how do they get home after midnight?
49. Do you know why the hospital was built here instead of staying in Vevey?
50. Have you seen the Google reviews (2.6★)? What do people complain about most?
51. Before HRC moved here, was it easier to get to the old hospital in Vevey?
52. Is there a sidewalk the whole way from Villeneuve station?

## Interview tips

- **Introduce yourself:** "We're architecture students from EPFL studying how people move around hospitals at night."
- **Ask permission before recording.** Most people are fine with it if you explain.
- **French.** All of this is in Romandie. Prepare key phrases:
  - "On étudie l'architecture à l'EPFL. On s'intéresse à comment les gens se déplacent la nuit autour des hôpitaux."
  - "Est-ce que vous travaillez de nuit ici ?"
  - "Comment vous rentrez chez vous après le dernier train ?"
  - "Est-ce qu'il y a un endroit pour attendre ou se reposer entre les horaires ?"
- **One quote is enough.** You don't need 10 interviews. One real person saying "I drive because there's nothing after midnight" is more powerful than any dataset.
- **Note: name (or "anonymous"), role, time of conversation, location.**
