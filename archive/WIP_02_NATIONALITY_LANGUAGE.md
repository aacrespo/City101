### The Lavaux Homogeneity Wall

The corridor drops from ~43% foreign (Lausanne) to 12.5% (Rivaz) — a 3.4× collapse — then rebounds to ~40% at Vevey. This is the same geography where:
- Shannon religious diversity drops to 0.0
- WCI collapses to near-zero
- Station richness falls to single digits
- Remote work infrastructure disappears entirely

**Rivaz (12.5% foreign) has 3.4× less nationality diversity than Geneva (50%) or Montreux (45.4%).** The Lavaux Fracture isn't just an infrastructure gap — it's a demographic cliff.

### Geneva: The Global Node

- 196 nationalities represented (per Genève Internationale)
- 41.5% foreign nationals (non-Swiss passport holders)
- But including binationaux: 66% of population has migration background
- 61% of population "issued from migration" (including naturalized Swiss)
- Top foreign communities: France (21% of foreign pop), Portugal (19%), Italy (11%), Spain (6%), Kosovo (4%)
- ~100,000 cross-border workers (frontaliers) daily — invisible in resident stats

### Vaud Canton Overview

- 855,700 total population (2024), 34% foreign
- Foreign communities: French (21%), Portuguese (18%), Italian (10%)
- Population growth: +1.1% in 2024, driven by immigration
- District of Aigle among fastest-growing (+1.7%/year over 5 years)

### Language Diversity (Vaud, 2023 OFS Relevé structurel)

- 82% report French as a principal language
- 9% report English (as principal language — highest after French)
- 7% report Portuguese
- 5% report German
- 39% of population aged 15+ report at least one non-French principal language
- 21% report multiple principal languages
- English spoken at work by 25% of Vaud residents — the professional lingua franca

### Religious Landscape (Vaud, 2023)

Major shifts over recent decade:
- Sans confession: 42% (up from 26% in 2010)
- Protestant: 18% (down from 29% in 2010)
- Catholic: remains significant but declining
- This secularization trend means the Shannon religious diversity index captures ACTIVE religious communities, not just nominal affiliation — making it a stronger signal of genuine social diversity

## Argument: Nationality Diversity Tracks Infrastructure Diversity

The corridor communes with highest foreign population % map almost exactly to the communes with highest WCI, Shannon, and station richness. The exceptions are instructive:

**Aigle (42.9% foreign, low WCI)**: High nationality diversity but at the corridor's eastern end with limited infrastructure. Diversity without accessibility = potential without realization.

**Founex (39.6% foreign, WCI near zero)**: Wealthy La Côte commune, many international residents (proximity to Geneva international orgs), but essentially car-dependent. Diversity exists but doesn't generate urban exchange because the infrastructure forces private mobility.

**Lavaux communes (12-27% foreign)**: The UNESCO vineyards select for a specific demographic — wealthy Swiss families, retired couples, heritage-conscious homeowners. The landscape's protection mechanisms (strict zoning, limited construction) create demographic homogeneity as a side effect. The absence of diversity isn't random — it's produced by the same forces that preserve the terraces.

## Connection to Comtesse's Formula

Diversity × Accessibility × Time = Urban Vitality

The corridor proves this in the negative: where nationality diversity drops (Lavaux), accessibility also drops (low train frequency, no workspaces), and temporal coverage shrinks (last train early, no evening economy). The three variables collapse together. They don't just correlate — they causally reinforce each other. Diverse populations generate demand for diverse services (restaurants, religious spaces, cultural venues), which in turn attract more diverse populations. The co-evolution ratchet.
