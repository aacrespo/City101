# The Medical Supply Chain — Full Corridor Map
## City101: Geneva → Villeneuve (101 km)
**Compiled:** 2026-03-15 (Cadence for Henna)
**Scope:** Every layer of the healthcare ecosystem along the corridor, not limited to specific nodes

---

## 1. THE CHAIN AT A GLANCE

The medical supply chain isn't a line — it's a web. At any given moment, the corridor sustains:

- **~46,000–48,000 healthcare workers** across all layers
- **~7,200 frontalier nurses** crossing from France daily
- **~10,000 EMS beds** requiring 24/7 staffing (Vaud + Geneva)
- **6 SMUR bases** + **14 ambulance bases** + **23–29 ambulances** operating across Canton Vaud
- **100,000 pharma packs/day** shipped from one distribution centre in Ecublens
- **1 truly 24h pharmacy** (Geneva) for the entire 101 km

During the **01:00–05:00 dead window**, an estimated **5,000–8,000 workers** are actively on shift — with essentially zero public transport.

## 8. LAYER 7 — THE FRONTALIER DIMENSION (the cross-border flow)

This data fundamentally changes the supply chain picture:

- **7,200 healthcare frontaliers** cross into Geneva + Vaud daily (nurses, midwives, aides-soignants)
- **17.6% of ALL nursing staff** in the franco-valdo-genevois region are frontaliers
- **Total nursing workforce in the zone:** 41,000 (Ain 9,700 + Haute-Savoie 15,300 + Geneva 4,600 + Vaud 11,100)
- **6,300 Haute-Savoie nurses** work in Geneva hospitals — 41% of all Haute-Savoie healthcare workers
- **60% of nurses in Geneva hospitals are French**
- In border communes: **up to 80%** of nurses work in Switzerland
- **+175% increase** in frontalier nurses toward Canton Vaud over 10 years
- **+65% increase** toward Canton Geneva over 10 years
- **90% of frontalier nurses** from Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes come from 3 zones: Genevois français (58%), Annecy (17%), Chablais (15%)
- **October 2024:** Geneva signed agreement to STOP active recruitment from French border hospitals
- **Salary differential:** French aide-soignant €1,650/month net vs Swiss median CHF 7,926/month gross

**What this means:** The healthcare supply chain doesn't just run along the corridor — it runs ACROSS the border. A nurse finishing at 02:00 at HUG may need to reach Annemasse. A CHUV nurse may live near Pontarlier. The dead window strands not just Swiss residents but the French workers who keep Swiss hospitals running.

## 12. NEWLY RESOLVED — Former gaps now filled

### Canton Vaud health sector total
- **81,900 jobs in 2021** = 17.2% of all cantonal employment. This is the macro number that validates the corridor estimate. Health is the single largest employment sector after services.

### CHUV FTE ratio
- 12,844 people for **10,185 FTE** (full-time equivalent) — ratio of 1.26. This means many part-time workers, which is structurally important: part-time nurses still need to commute during off-peak hours.

### Night shift structure (indirect evidence)
- CHUV union documents confirm: "Dans service 24/24, tout le monde fait les horaires dans la même proportion" — in 24h services, everyone does equal proportions of night shifts
- Night shift dispensation requires medical certificate renewed every 3 months
- Maximum 50h/week including nights
- Typical Swiss hospital nursing shifts are ≤8 hours (not 12h like Ireland/UK)
- **Estimated night shift calculation:** If CHUV has ~5,257 nurses across 24h services, and shifts are 8h with 3 rotations, roughly **1,750 nurses are on any given night shift** at CHUV alone. Add doctors, logistics, cleaning, security, kitchen, and the number approaches **2,500–3,000 per night** at CHUV.
- HUG with 13,086 employees: similar ratio would give **3,000–4,000 per night**

### Hospital catering (now documented)
- **CHUV kitchen:** produces ~5,000 meals per day (lunches and dinners). Pre-dawn preparation confirmed for breakfast service
- **HUG Catering Service:** produces **10,000+ meals per day** — not just for HUG patients/staff but also for IMAD, EMS, and schools in Geneva. Uses 142 tonnes of GRTA-certified vegetables/year
- Both hospitals require overnight kitchen staff for breakfast preparation and overnight patient meals

### Medical gas supply (now documented)
- **Carbagas Healthcare** (Air Liquide subsidiary): #1 medical gas supplier in Switzerland. Part of Air Liquide Healthcare (15,000 employees, 30 countries, 20,000+ hospitals/care centres, 2.3 million patients)
- **Linde/PanGas** (formerly PanGas, renamed Linde 2024): second major supplier
- **Hospital oxygen delivery:** Large hospitals (CHUV, HUG) use liquid oxygen in stationary tanks with **automated remote monitoring and tanker truck delivery** — Carbagas operates its own fleet of specialised trucks
- **Ligue pulmonaire suisse:** Maintains oxygen refill stations across Switzerland including Lausanne and Geneva for home patients with portable liquid oxygen systems
- **Home oxygen therapy (VitalAire):** Carbagas subsidiary VitalAire provides home oxygen equipment and regional patient supply — requires delivery/maintenance visits
- **Critical point:** Medical gas delivery by tanker truck runs on road schedules independent of public transport — but the WORKERS at delivery depots, hospital gas management technicians, and VitalAire home visit staff are all part of the night/early morning workforce

### EMS staffing data (path identified)
- **SOMED data is downloadable** from OFS as Excel files: "Statistique des institutions médico-sociales 2024 – Tableaux standard" (138 kB xlsx). Contains personnel data by canton, establishment type, domain of activity
- **INFOSAN Vaud** (infosan.vd.ch) offers an **interactive pivot table** for EMS personnel exploration — can filter by region, activity domain, year. This is the tool to get exact EMS staffing numbers along the corridor
- **Obsan monitoring 2025:** Vaud has 18.5% nursing staff exit rate (below Swiss average). Geneva 12.3% (lowest in Switzerland). This means corridor healthcare workforce is relatively STABLE compared to German-speaking cantons (Aargau 30.3%, Bern 28.5%)
- **Frontalier temp nursing authorizations:** 548 in 2014 → 904 in 2024 nationally — growing reliance on temporary cross-border staff

### Ambulance coverage detail (now documented)
- **6 SMUR units** in Canton Vaud, attached to acute care hospitals, **24h/24**
- **Ambulance Riviera (La Tour-de-Peilz):** 35 professional paramedics, ~5,000 missions/year. **3–4 crews by day, 2 crews at night.** Covers 100,000 population across 9 communes
- **EHC CSU Morges-Aubonne:** 26 staff, 2 ambulances + 1 SMUR vehicle. 2 bases (Morges + Aubonne). ~3,500 missions/year. SMUR covers Rolle → Lausanne + pied du Jura. 600+ SMUR missions/year with 8–10 different ambulance companies
- **CSU-NVB (Nord Vaudois-Broye):** 120+ staff, 5 bases, 2 24h ambulances + transfer vehicles. ~9,500 missions/year. (Outside corridor but shows system scale)
- **TCS Ambulance Vaud:** Based in Villars-Sainte-Croix, 24h/24 via 144 dispatch

## 13. WHAT REMAINS GENUINELY UNAVAILABLE

These items cannot be resolved through web research alone:

1. **Exact night shift headcount per hospital** — not published in annual reports. The CHUV "Données essentielles" PDF has an "Effectifs et démographie" section but only total headcount, gender ratio, FTE, and nationality breakdown. Night/day split would require internal HR data or direct institutional request.

2. **Ambulance response time data by location and hour** — exists inside the Centrale 144 SAGA system but is not public. The DisCUP reform documents reference performance metrics but don't publish them. This data would prove whether the dead window has measurable impact on emergency response.

3. **Commune-level healthcare worker commute patterns** — the OFS Strukturerhebung (structural survey) collects commute data but published tables aggregate by district, not commune. The Observatoire transfrontalier has detailed data for the French side but only aggregate Swiss data. Getting commune-level: would require a custom data request to OFS or Statistique Vaud.

4. **Exact Galexis Ecublens headcount** — Galexis reports 840 total across both centres (Niederbipp + Ecublens). The Ecublens split is not published. Given that Ecublens is described as the "second-largest location" and Niederbipp as the primary, a reasonable estimate is 300–400 at Ecublens.

5. **Medical waste logistics** — hospitals generate regulated medical waste requiring specialised collection (sharps, biological, chemical, radioactive). This runs on scheduled pickups including early morning. Companies like Saubermacher or Cand-Landi handle this in Romandie, but specific routes/times along the corridor are not publicly documented.

6. **Hospital security staffing** — all major hospitals have 24/7 security. CHUV and HUG have internal security teams plus contracted guards. Numbers not published. Likely 20–50 security staff per major hospital on night shift.

7. **Hospital IT/technical on-call** — CHUV employs a CIO and technical teams. SignalHire data shows 1% of CHUV staff have <1 year tenure, 28% have >11 years — suggesting a stable technical core. But night on-call IT/technical staffing numbers are not published.

8. **Private GP on-call network** — the CTMG (Centrale téléphonique des médecins de garde, 0848 133 133) coordinates on-call GPs across Vaud. These doctors are on standby overnight but the number of GPs participating and their geographic distribution is managed internally by the Société vaudoise de médecine.
