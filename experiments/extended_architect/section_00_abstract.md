# 0. Abstract

The architect's core competence is integration — resolving structure, envelope, program, sustainability, circulation, buildability, and experience simultaneously. Two structural limits constrain this: a cognitive ceiling on simultaneous constraint-handling, and an economic model that places specialist knowledge at project phases where its influence is marginal. AI agents carrying domain knowledge collapse the cost of early multidisciplinary presence, making it viable to have structural, buildability, and sustainability knowledge active from first sketch rather than first coordination meeting. This transforms the organigram — the diagram of project roles, hierarchies, and communication flows — from an administrative artifact into a tunable design instrument, with three independently configurable dimensions: communication topology, context scope, and task dependency. The consequence is architectural: materials and construction methods currently too niche to propose become accessible to explore; buildings are shaped by the simultaneity of constraints rather than the sequence of consultations; and the architect's role elevates from coordinator to system designer — practicing direction générale as a design act. The framework is grounded in six months of AI-augmented architectural practice (City101, EPFL Studio Huang), a demonstrated multi-agent build (7 agents, 709 objects in shared CAD space), and a three-system architecture (knowledge management, agent organization, execution infrastructure) whose logic is established and whose full implementation is the next step.
